# **​1​ Major Suit Openings**

We play 5-card majors (could be 4 in 3rd/4th seat etc.)  Our general style is 2/1 GF unless suit rebid (or unless opener shows extras).  Because we have a strong forcing opening, our 1M openers are limited to 11-17 HCP.

Note that holding a 5- or 6-card major and a *longer* minor suit, our systemic opening is 1M.  Theoretically opener could choose to treat it as 4M+longer minor and open 1♣ instead.  Fortunately these hand patterns are rare.

## **​1.1​ Major suit raises**

In general our system over 1♠ is the same as over 1♥ except the bids are one step higher. Summary is, over 1M:

* 2M is a simple 3 or 4 card raise, 7-10 in support of the suit  
* 2M+1 is a mini-splinter.  These show two types of hands.  The first is a limit raise where you want opener to evaluate opposite your shortness.   The second is a slammish hand which exceeds the minimum GF slinter.   
* 2M+2 is similar to Jacoby 2NT.  It’s GF with 4 card support.  
* 2M+3 (3M-2) is a limit raise with no shortness   
* 3M-1 is a constructive 4 card raise  
* 3M is a 4 card preemptive raise   
* 3NT is 13-15 balanced with 3 card support  This takes care of awkward bid in our system.  It is difficult to make a 2/1 GF bid, show 3 card support and convey a balanced hand.  
* 4M-2 is a constructive raise with 5 cards.   After this:  
  * 4M-1 is shortness asking. 4M is no shortness, then cheaper/lower,   
  * 4M+1 is RKC  
  * 4M+2+ asks for first/second round control.   

Over a 1♥ opening:

* 1♥\-3♠ is an unspecified splinter (relay, cheaper/lower) limited by the fact that we also have an alternate splinter sequence available to show strong slam interest.  4M is to play,   
* 1♥\-4♣ is a 5 card constructive raise.    
* 1♥\-4♦  is Namyats for Spades.  4-4.5 losers

Over a 1♠ opening:

* 1♠-4♣ a minor suit splinter (relay to ask, cheaper/lower)  
* 1♠-4♦ is a 5-card constructive raise  
* 1♠-4♥ is a splinter in hearts.


### 

Over a 1M-2M single raise, we play 7-way game tries:

* 2M+1 is an unspecified help suit game try  
* 2M+2 to 2M+4 are short suit game tries, cheaper/lower.     
* 3M is a quantitative game try 

Suit bids beyond 3M after a 1M opening are rare since we play limited openings but they show distributional jump-shift strength hands (4- losers)).    

This system applies any time one partner makes a 2M raise and the next opponent passes. Over interference our game tries are as follows:

* Over 3M-1, we play a Maximal Overcall Double--that is X is the only game try, and says nothing about penalizing the opponents.    
* Over 3M-2, 3M-1 is the only game try and double is optional.   
* Over X of 2M, our game tries still apply and XX is natural  
* Over other bids, bids below 3M are game tries, and as best they can for that situation describe what responder is looking for. 

### **​1.1.2​ Mini Splinters**

The cheapest relay 2M+2 asks for the suit. Responses are:

* 2M+3/2M+4 show shortness in ♣/♦.  Responder still can have the strong hand.   
* 3M shows a limit raise with shortness in the other major.     
* Bids beyond 3M show the strong hand with shortness in the other major. They are mostly natural, with 3NT showing equal strength in the other two suits, and 4♣ showing a hand such as AJxxx x QT AQJTx. Explicitly bidding the other major shows a void.  

Bids of 3M-1, 3M-2 are help-suit retries.  A singleton is not help.   A void may be help depending on the rest of the hand.   

Rebids of 3M or 4M show opener is equally sad or happy opposite any mini-splinter,  AQJxx Kxx Kx Kxx is a sample 4♠  bid.

### **​1.1.3​ Jacoby-like 2M+2**

2M+2 is similar to Jacoby, GF without shortness.  Just like Jacoby, it’s a shortness asking bid.  

* 3M-2 \= no shortness or Opener just wants captaincy. Responder describes naturally:  
  * 3NT is 4333\.  4♣ is a control ask, 0-3, 4, 5, etc.    
  * 3M is some 5332 or longer suit.  3M+1 asks for the doubleton 3M+2 asks for controls.     
  * Suits are 4+.  Opener can bid RKC, relay to ask for controls (0-3 is the first step), or bid at the 5 level to ask for 2nd round control.      
* 3M-1, 3M, 3M+1 \= shortness in clubs/diamonds/major.  3M+1 is specifically a stiff.  
* 3NT/1♥ is specifically a spade void.  
* 4Suit \= 5-5, natural.  Doesn’t promist extras, just bidding out shape.  Relay is shortness asking, structure like in jump-shifts.   4♠ specifically shows 56 shape in the majors  as 55 is typically opened 1♠  
* 4M  is fast arrival, shows (1) minimal values, (2) no shortness, and (3) 0-3 controls.  


### **1.1.4 1M-4M-2 Five Card Constructive Raise** 

This bid is usually made with a 5 card raise with enough values for slam if opener has the right values.   Axxxx x KJxx xxx for instance, something short of a full splinter.   With KQxxx Axx AQJx x obviously slam can make.   Response structure is similar to Namyats:

4M-1:  Asks for shortness

* 4M no shortness.    
* 4M+1 shows a void  
* 4M+2 to 4M+4 show singletons in that suit.   

4M+1 is RKC.

4M+2 to 4M+4 asks for control in a side suit (cheaper/lower)

* Step 1 is no 1st or 2nd round.  Relay asks for 3rd round: none, xx, Q.      
* Step 2 is 2nd round control.   Relay asks:  Stiff, King, KQ+  
* Step 3 is 1st round control.   Relay asks, Void, Ace  
* Step 4 is both 1st and 2nd round, Relay asks:  Stiff A, AK

## **​1.2​ Forcing NT**

In GCC events we play 1NT forcing (semi-forcing by passed hand).  1♥-1♠-1NT can be a balanced 12\+\-15\- that opener did not want to open with a weak NT; he must bid 2NT to show a maximum hand.  New suits by responder at the 2-level are misfit signoffs.  New suits at the 3-level are non-forcing but constructive/invitational.

In mid-chart events we use the Kleinman Inversion *in response to 1st/2nd seat 1M openings only* \-- 1♥\-1NT shows 4+ spades, 1♥\-1♠ denies 4 spades.  1♥\-1♠-1NT is still balanced 12\+\-15\-.  In response to 3rd/4th seat openings we revert to a fairly natural system; the salient feature is that P-1♥\-1♠ is natural and *non*\-forcing.

When the inversion is used, 1♠  does not contain any GF hands except for possibly 3244 minimum GF hands (will bid 3NT next).   Over 1♠, we also try to exclude GF hands, bidding 2/1 instead and then 2NT on the balanced hands.  

Over a 2NT rebid by opener over a forcing 1NT, we play 3M as forcing, choice of game.   We don’t typically bid 1NT with a weak hand with 3 card support (either bid 2M or pass) so 1NT followed by 3M here is a balanced hand that would have made a limit raise.  3m is invitational,  with a 9-10 point hand shy of bidding    
 

## **​1.3​ 2/1 Game Force Unless Suit Rebid** 

We play a 2/1 bid showing a game forcing hand unless responder rebids his suit.  Game forcing means we do not stop at 4m.    Opener has the following rebids:

* 2NT showing 12-15 balanced.   Responder may rebid his minor suit as non-forcing, but 1♠-2♥\-2NT-3♥ is game forcing since responder can count on two card heart support.    
* Any new suit at the 2-level does not show extras.  This includes 1♥\-2m-2♠.  
* 2M denies other rebids, in the sequence 1M-2♣-2M specifically, there is a strong inference  opener has 6, in other sequences he may not be strong enough to reverse into the 4 card suit at the 3 level.  
* 3 of responder’s suit is 3+, forcing but doesn’t need to show much extra strength since a fit exists  
* 3 of a new suit is game forcing...   
* 3M shows 6+, semisolid suit and 15-17.     
* A jump shift into a new suit is a splinter in support of responder’s suit.     
* 3NT is 15+-17, balanced, which of course means at least two cards in responder’s suit. 


## **​1.4​ Jump Shift Rebids**

A jump shift rebid by opener shows minimum 55 shape and a 4 loser hand.   This applies after both a 1/1 and 2/1 response.  We also have the characteristic that the lower suit could be longer if it is a minor.  **We may optionally play the system on Relays and Asks.**   

4M+1 is kickback after any of these bids, if responder has spades after 1♥-1NT-3m, responder should just bid 3♠

If responder bids 3 of a new suit lower than M after a 1♠ or 1NT response, this simply shows a long suit and less than GF values originally, and less than invitational values in a forcing NT sequence. In these less than invitational sequences, Opener may pass if it looks like a misfit, no stoppers for 3NT.

3♠ after responder shows 4+ spades is just a one round force with 6+, either looking for 4♠ or 3NT.  4 of opener’s minor is the least encouraging bid.   3♠ after responder denies 4+ spades is forcing asking for help in spades (min Qx).   

## **​1.5​ Passed Hand Responses**

We play Drury, see the section on Position Considerations.  

## **​1.6​ Interference** 

Over X (whether a power double or takeout) we play:

* XX implies no fit 9+,  \\  
* 1NT is natural 6-9, denies a fit.    
* 1♠ shows 4+ spades and is forcing for one round  
* 2NT is Jordan, LR+  
* New suits at the 2-level are non-forcing 5+ 

Over a simple overcall, bids roughly  terms of priority.  

* Jump bids below 4M are fit showing.  Showing 8+ cards in the two suits,  There is no requirement on strength, could be GF, LR (if below the 3M level), or competitive, mostly asking partner to evaluate opposite a fit.   
* Cue bids are LR+ with 3+ support,   
* 2M is a simple raise   
* 3M is preemptive, with a constructive 4 card raise hand make a simple raise and compete to 3M.  
* 4M is to play.  
* A jump cue is a splinter.    
* 1NT/(1♠) is 7-10 and natural with a stopper.   
* 2NT is natural 11-12 with a stopper in their suit  
* X is negative, 8+, shows support for the other two suits if there is an unbid major implies 4oM because of the rest of the structure.  It could also show 5oM and too weak a hand to bid 2M. 2oM or 3 in new suit after doubling is not forcing.    
* Non-jumps in new suits are forcing for one round, 5+.in the suit, generally 10+   They tend to deny a fit  since we have fit showing jumps.  They also tend to deny 4 in the unbid major They generally do not promise a rebid--to make a forcing bid opener should Qbid to show support or jump.  

Alternatively, we may also choose to play Optional doubles, 8+ with cards in their suit   

* The cheapest NT bid is a takeout for the other two suits. 

This variation is to be done by discussion at the table, often to counter aggressive overcallers.   

Over a weak jump shift, new suits are forcing.  Jumps are still fit showing if below 4M. :

### **​1.6.1​ Unusual/Unusual**

Over a Known 2 suiter (Includes Unusual 2NT/Roman Jumps, cuebids with specific suites, we play Unusual/Unusual where the **cheaper cuebid shows support.**   The choice really doesn’t matter over the eponymous 2NT bid, but over Roman jumps it may matter.   Over for example 1♥-(2♠) showing Spades and Clubs  we want the 3♣ bid to show a LR+ in hearts, and the 3♠ bid to be forcing in diamonds. In general if only one of the cuebids is below 3M we want to make that one support.  

* The cheaper cuebid is LR+ in the major.  Bids below 3M are invites.   3M+1 is not Non-Serious since we have an 8 card fit   
* The more expensive cuebid is GF in the unbid suit.   This sets up a forcing pass situation.      
* Bidding the unbid suit is thus constructive and non-forcing.   
* Raising the major to 3M, is a competitive/constructive raise, anything short of a limit raise.   
* Jumping in the unbid suit if below 4M is still fit showing. 

# **​2​ 1NT Systems**

We are specifically referring to the sequences where a 1NT bid or rebid shows a balanced hand in a specific range.   This excludes 1NT responses and the 1NT takeout overcall over their 1-bids for example, but it includes the 1NT 15-17 balanced hand in the sequence (1A)-P-(1B)-1NT.   The sequences include:

1. 1NT opening showing  11\+\-14 HCP.  
2. 1NT non-vul 3d seat showing 8-13 HCP   
3. 1♣-1♦-1NT showing 15-17.  
4. 1♣-1M-1NT showing 15-17, denying 4 in the major.  
5. 1♦-1♥-1NT showing 18-20, balanced.     
6. 1♦-1♠-1NT showing 18-20 balanced.   
7. 1♥-1♠-1NT showing 11\+\-15\- balanced.  
8. 4th seat overcall as described above   
9. 4th seat balancing 1NT showing 14-17 

We like bidding 1NT, not only do we show a narrow range with these bids, it often affords us the opportunity to declare 1NT.  All of these have specific structures to cater to, for example for (2) and (5) we are primarily interested in signing off--and can’t even play systems over (2).   For (4) and (6) we know we have the majority of points are primarily interested in constructive bidding and not interested in runouts or signoffs.   For the others both weak and strong hands for responder need to be considered.

## **​2.2​ Weak 1NT(1) opening**

Our 1NT opening shows 11\+\-14 HCP balanced, sometimes with 5cM.  Could also have a 6-card minor, something like 2362\.  We play 2-way Stayman, with the 2♦ GF bid for most GF hands except ones with 10 cards in the majors, or seven cards in one major.   We cater the system toward signoffs and invites.  

### **​2.2.1​ Forcing Stayman 2♦**

This establishes a game force, and all forcing pass conventions apply.   Partner is not necessarily interested in slam but will have some choices.  We recommend this option for game force hands with some exceptions with 10 cards in the majors, since it sets forcing pass sequences and clarifies some sequences.   An exception may be when you can pick the contract immediately.   For example bid 2♦ with KQxx Kxx AQJT xx but 2♣ with KQxx Kxx xx AQJT to avoid lead directing doubles.   

2M promises at least 4 cards in the OTHER major.   If you have both majors, bid 2♥ to show spades first then 3♥  if asked again.   After 2M:

* An immediate 4C jump over a suit is RKC Gerber.   
* 2♠ or 3♥/2♠ establishes the suit as trump and shows slam interest, inviting partner to cuebid.     
* 2NT asks for another 4 card suit (Baron).   Opener bids the suit naturally at the 3 level, or rebids his major (naturally) with 5, or bids 3NT with 4333 shape.  If responder bids a suit after this it establishes trump and show slam interest.     
* 3 of a new suit shows a game forcing hand with 5+ in that suit. 3oM does not necessarily show slam interest, may be a choice of game.   This shows some of the weakness of the methods.   Opener should cuebid if that applies.    
* 3NT is to play,  We can assume responder has 4+ in the other major.   
* An immediate 4♣ is RKC gerber if that applies.      
* 4NT, 5NT/2♥ is quantitative.   If you want to bid RKC, bid your suit to establish trump first.  
* Jump shifts are splinters when possible.   

3m promises at least 5 in the OTHER minor (sometimes opener is 6322).   With exactly 5, opener should still normally make this bid, with exceptions being a weak hand and a 5 card suit.  

* 3♦ agrees diamonds as trump and is slammish.   Opener usually cuebids a major or bids 3NT as discouraging,     
* 3M suit shows 5+ in the suit bid and sets trump.   3M is possibly choice of game, if opener has a 3 card fit and slam interest he cuebids.     
* 3NT is to play   
* 4♣/3♦ is natural and agrees club as trump.     
* 4m shows a slammish hand with the minor.  This is awkward but we want an immediate 3m bid to be weak,   
* 4♦/3♣ We can make up a specific meaning for this.     
* 4M is signoff--this sequence shouldn’t really exist since we have many ways to show the major, but I am open to assigning a conventional meaning here. 

2NT denies a 4cM or 5cm. Responder bids 3 suit naturally to set the suit as trump, Slammish if the suit is a minor.  Opener then cuebids in support of the suit with 3 card support, or 3NT.   3M then 4M is more encouraging than 4M directly.   4♦ is slammish with both minors, opener bids 4M with fit in corresponding minor (which he must have).    

If the opponents double 2♦, all responses are the same, except XX shows diamonds and 3♣ shows clubs.   Pass generally shows the same response as 2NT but shows 4 diamonds.   If they overcall,   we no longer invert the majors, bids are natural, double shows 4+ in the major bid.   


### **​2.2.2​ Stayman 2♣** 

This is generally our only way to invite, so some sequences have meanings other than the usual interpretation.  We also put various distributional hands with both majors, since those hands are so hard to bid after GF Stayman.   

2♦ Denies 4cM.  Opener should not bid 3m with a 5+ card minor,  

* 2M here is invitational with 5+ in the major.  Note that 2♥ is not weak and signoff with both majors.   Opener may pass, or re-invite with 2NT or 3M.  Opener should simply pass 2M with a minimum hand and two card support and not bid 2NT.    
* 2NT/3NT are invitational/signoff.   They do show 4 in at least one major.    
* 3m is invitational with that minor.    
* 3♥/3♠ are invitational/game forcing with 55 in the majors.   
* 4M show 46/64 shape with the major bid.  
* 4m show the same thing as 4M except with slam interest.    

2M shows 4+ in that major.   Generally all bids are similar, over 

* 2NT/3NT opener can assume responder has 4 in oM, and should correct to that strain.    
* 3♥/2♠ is invitational in hearts.  Another weakness in the system  
* 3M shows a fit and is invitational.  Of course we are aware it shows a narrow range since 2M could have been passed and responder could just decide to bid 4M.    
* 3♠/2♥ or 4♥/3♠ show the 64 hands.    
* 4m is a splinter with slam interest, showing both majors.   (Could be 64 or 55).   

If Stayman is doubled, generally make the bid you would normally make, except XX and 2♦ deny a 5cM and show a good 4 or 5+ card suit.  Pass simply denies 4cM.   

### **​2.2.3​ Other Responses** 

Responses of 2M are natural and non-forcing.  Opener is permitted to raise freely based on degree of fit, LOTT etc., after which responder is permitted to re-evaluate; thus the sequence 1NT-2M-3M-4M is infrequent but does exist.

3-level bids are also natural and non-forcing (but 1NT-3M tends to be more distributional than 1NT-2M).  Responder’s strength can be wide-ranging, anything from pre-emptive to just short of invitational.

1NT-3NT is to play.

1NT-4M is a signoff.  Again, this is a wide-ranging bid, anything from pre-emptive to a hand with traditional GF strength (but not so strong that responder worries about missing slam).

1NT-4m is South African Transfer.  Follows the same convention as Namyats Opener bids the intermediate step with mild slam interest, after which responder signs off with a minimum hand or shows shortness (cheaper/lower, 5M without shortness) otherwise.  Kickback is still Kickback.  
 

### **​2.2.4​ Runouts**

When 1NT is doubled for penalty, all suit bids are natural and non-forcing.  The one quirk is 1NT-(X)-2♣.  This is non-forcing and promises either (a) 5+ clubs or (b) a hand with two or three 4-card suits outside of clubs.

1NT-(X)-P is “natural” and suggests sitting for 1NT doubled, either based on values, or even with a broke hand hoping that 1NTX is the best spot.  If this passes around to opener, opener may redouble to show a good hand with a 5-card or longer suit.  Responder may then judge to sit for 1NTXC, or bid 2♣ to escape to opener’s suit.

Over a balancing double 1NT-(P)-P-(X), opener’s immediate XX means the same thing as in the above paragraph.

### **​2.2.5​ Interference**

Over 2♣, we simply play Systems on, X=Stayman, 2♦ is GF Stayman, this is our exception to Rubensohl.  Specifically if 2♣ shows both majors, we play X is penalty oriented, and Unusual/Unusual.  2NT/4NT is both monors.,  Over 2♦-2♠ natural we play Rubensohl, described in our Competitive Auctions.   

## **​2.3​ 3rd Seat NV 1NT(2) and 1♦-1♥-1NT(5) opening**

We treat these two openings similarly, since responder wants to run out and choose a suit, 

* 2 suit is natural,  we suggest responder take out even to a weak suit.  Opener can super-accept by bidding anything else (or redoubling a double).   
* 2NT shows the minors 55 and is not invitational.   This applies even after a double or an overcall.     
* 3 suit in the 1♦ case shows 55 or longer (56) in suit and one above.  

If they double,  XX is run out to 4 card suits up the line where allowed.   In 3rd seat NV only, we open 1NT on 8-13 balanced.  Due to the wide range we are not permitted *any* conventional calls following this opening, even if the opponents double or intervene.

## **​2.4​ 15-17 1♣\-1♦\-1NT Rebid (3)**

This shows 15-17 balanced.   We know responder cannot have 8+ with a 4+cM, so our constructive bidding is concentrated on the minor suits.   We use a three way puppet system--2♣, 2♦, and 2♥ are puppets to the next bids but also tend to show stronger hands with clubs, diamonds, or both.    We have a system where we actually have many more sequences than usual we can devote to constructive sequences,  The challenge here is to have something easy to remember for the  Opener is allowed to super-accept.  

* 2NT, 4NT, 5NT are invitational to 3,6, and 7 respectively.    
* 2♣, 2♥ is weak with at least 5 hearts and 4 spades.   
* 2m, 2NT is invitational with that minor,  Kxx xx AJxxx xxx  
* 2m, 2♠ is invitational with 5m \+ 4om  
* 2m, 3m  is forcing with 5+ in the minor, denies other minor  
* 2m, 3M is a splinter denying other minor thus must be 6+   
* 2m, 3om is forcing with 6+ in the first minor, 4 in om  
* 3m  directly is a invitational with 6+   
* 2♥, 3m is forcing with 5+ in the minor bid, 4 in the other minor   
* 2♥, 2NT shows 55+ in minors, invitational  
* 2♥, 3M is 55 with shortness in the major  
* 2♠ in Minor Suit Stayman usually showing 44+ in both minors.    
* 3m directly is invitational with 6+  
* 3M shows a solid 7 card minor,    
* 3NT is to play

## **​2.5​ 15-17 1♣\-1M-1NT Rebid(4)**

Opener has denied 4 card support for the major bid.   We play 2-way Checkback Stayman in a similar way with our Weak NT system.         

### **​2.5.1​ Forcing Stayman 2♦**

Opener will show either 3 card support for M or 4 in oM.  Opener will choose to bid 2♥ with both features.   This is in the interest of space, allowing responder to bid 2♠.  3m by opener denies either feature and shows a 5 card minor   We do not switch the 2M or 3m responses here.

After 2M:

* 2♠ shows 4 spades if 1M=1♥,  and 5+ spades if 1M=1♠.  Opener bids 3♠ with support, otherwise 2NT denies support and 3m denies support with a 5cm.     
* 2NT asks for shape clarification, opener should bid 4+ in a minor as before.

3m shows the minor and is forcing, shows 4 in the orginal major bid and a longer minor.  If opener had just 4 in the major, he would have bid 3m directly.       

* 3M agrees major as suit and is slammish,   Opener cuebids.      
* jump bids are splinters when possible.   The sequence 1♣-1♠; 1NT-2♦; 2♥-3♠; shows some 64 hand.   

A 2NT:  bid implies 2344 shape.    

* 3m shows 4+ in the minor bid, sets the suit as trump.     
* 3M shows 6+ in the suit bid and is forcing   
* 3NT is to play

 A 3m bid shows 5,  responder cuebids a major in support, or bids 3NT or 4 of the other minor,    

### **​2.5.2​ Checkback Stayman 2♣** 

This is the way we do most invites.   Responses are similar to 2♣ after our weak NT.   After 2♦, our responses are as follows: 

* 2M is invitational with 6  
* 2NT is natural and invitational   
* 2oM is a distributional invite.     
* 3m is invitational with a longer minor

After 2M, bids are similar, with 3M being the invitational bid in the major.  

### **​2.5.3​ Other bids** 

* 1♠ then 2♥ shows 55 in the majors, invitational   
* 1♠ then 3♥ or 1♥ then 2♠  show 55 hands and is forcing  
* 2M is a minimum, usually with 6\.   Opener has already denied a max with 3 card support so it’s usually passed.    
* 2NT, 4NT, 5NT are invitational to 3, 6, and 7 respectively.    
* 3m directly shows 45+ in the major and minor bid, game forcing.  
* 3M is forcing with 6+, usually choice of game, but opener can show slam interest by bidding a new suit.   3♠ asks for shortness (3N=none).      
* 3NT is to play 

## **​2.6​ 18-20 1♦-1♠-1NT Rebid (6)**

This bid is made systemically with a 5cM, since the alternative is a 2M bid. To cater to this possibility we play a version of Puppet Stayman advocated by Kit Woolsey.   Since some bridge players refer to this as Puppy Stayman, we affectionately call ours Kitty Stayman.   This is the bid you would make with 4 cards in either major, or a strong hand with a minor.   This includes 54 and 64 major suit hands (bid 3oM on the next bid).   The rest of the system is pretty traditional: transfers,  Minor Suit Stayman, as well as South African Transfers to transfer captaincy back to the strong hand   

### **​2.6.1​ Woolsey Puppet 2♣ Stayman (Kitty Stayman)**

Opener only bids 2M with a 5 card major.   Hence responder only makes this bid with a signoff hand in diamonds if he has tolerance for both majors.   This system is not geared toward signoff hands as junk Stayman or other bids would be better.    
In response to 2♦ denying a 5cM:

* 2M shows 4 in the other major and at least invitational strength.  2NT is the weakest rebid, denying a fit or strength.   Opener rebids 2♠ or 3♥/2♠ with a fit and a minimum hand, and 3♠ or at the 4 level with a fit and max.  With a max and no fit opener can bid 3m or 3NT  
* 2NT shows an invitational hand with both 44 suits.   
* 3m is GF and 5+ cards in the minor bid.     
* 3M shows a GF hand with 4 cards in M and 5+ cards in oM.    ‘  
* 3NT shows 44 in both majors.  This is a switch in meaning from the original, since people wanted to bid Stayman with 33 in the majors.   With 3325 you can bid 3m here.  With 3343 like just respond 3NT originally.    
* 4m shows 44 in both majors and slam interest 

In response to 2M:

* 2♠ or 3♥/2♠ shows at least a 3 card fit and is game forcing   
* 2NT is invitational, certainly deying a 4 card fit   
* 3m is GF 5+ in the minor bid.   
* 3M is invitational   
* 3NT, 4M are signoff,  3NT does show 4 in oM  
* jump shifts are splinters when possible.  

### **​2.6.2​ Major Suit Transfers**

## **​2.7​ 15-17 Sandwich 1NT overcall (8)**

## **​2.12​ Other Interference**

Over 1NT-(2♣) when 2♣ shows the majors, we play Unusual-vs-Unusual type cue-bids, and both 2NT and 4NT bids show the minors.  Over any other meaning of 1NT-(2♣), we play that X replaces the 2♣ bid and all other systems are on.  

Otherwise we play a Rubensohl-style system: 2-level bids are natural and non-forcing, 2NT is a Lebensohl-style puppet to 3♣ (could be signoff, invitational, or Stayman with a stopper), and 3-level bids are transfers into the next higher suit (transferring into a known enemy suit is Staymanish without a stopper).  Doubles are typically penalty at the 2-level and negative at the 3-level (but might be altered depending on the exact meaning of the interference).

## **​2.13​ Passed Hand Responses**

In response to a 4th seat 1NT, or a 3rd seat vulnerable 11-14, 2♣ is still Stayman, while 2♦ is natural and non-forcing.  Our agreements over interference are all suits natural/non-forcing and 2NT natural invitational.

# **​3​ Multi 2♦ opening**

We have agreed to play 2**♦** in Midchart events to represent a weak 2M opening or a 17-20 HCP 4441 opening with any shortness, or 5440 with the length in a minor.   It should be around a 5-loser hand, that should be a criterion for upgrading or downgrading, AKxx QJTx AQxx x  \- or AJxx AQxx AKJx Q etc.  This means that any 1NT rebid after a 1♦ opening shows a balanced hand.  

## **​3.1​ Responses to 2♦**

2M, 3M, 4♥ are Pass/Pull.   Partner passes if he has the particular preempt in the suit.   As a strategic procedure,  Partner may bid 2M if he suspects the strong hand especially if both opponents have passed.   In almost all cases, we can signoff at the 3 level in one of partner’s 4 card suit. If partner has the strong hand he bids the bottom of the three touching suits, with NT being the 3-suited hand hardest to bid for example:

Over 2♥:

* P shows a weak 2 in hearts  
* 2♠ shows a weak 2 in spades  
* 2N shows heart shortness (4144 or 4045/4054, this is true for other bids)  
* 3♣ shows spade shortness (the shortness is always below the suit bid)  
* 3♦ shows club shortness  
* 3♥ shows diamond shortness

Over 2♠:

* 2N shows club shortness , since a 3♥ bid would show hearts.  
* 3♣ shows spade shortness  
* 3♦ shows club shortness  
* 3♥ shows a heart preempt  
* 3♠ shows 4144

The more usual treatment s to bid 3♣ with 4414, and have the relay be a general invite.  However,  we have a narrow range for our 4441’s so we can use the cuebid for slam going hands.

Over the 4441 rebids if responder rebids 3NT it’s to play.  Cuebid of the short suit is the forward going bid showing slam interest.   We can further develop this but for now opener bids up the line.  4NT or 5NT by responder are quantitative and invitational to 6 and 7 respectively.  

A 2NT bid is an asking bid, inquiring about the strength and suit of the weak two.  It shows at least invitational values hence would have some interest in slam vs the 4441\.   Rebids:

* 3♣/3♦ shows a good hand in the corresponding major.   If responder rebids three of the major, it’s still signoff (he probably had game interest in the other major. 4♣ by responder is our keycard sequence.   The step response is shortness asking, with the 3M bid showing no shortness,   The next three steps are cheaper/lower.    
* 3♥/3♠ shows a bad hand with the major bid.  4♣ is keycard,  3♠ /3♥ is shortness asking,  4 of the other major or 5 of a minor or 3N is to play,     
* 3N, 4+ shows the 4441 type hands.  

A 3m or 3N bid is to play.   If opener has 4441, opener rebids as usual, and it will usually be the suit above 3m.  

## **​3.2​ If the Opponents Bid**

If the opponents play multi, we adopt option 2 of the ACBL recommend defense:    
[http://web2.acbl.org/defensedatabase/mc20.pdf](http://web2.acbl.org/defensedatabase/mc20.pdf)

After bids not specifically showing a 5 card major, bids of 2M, 3M, 4♥ are still pass/pull.   This includes bids showing a takeout of a specific major.   Doubles by responder are generally penalty, inviting partner to double.   

## **​3.3​ Opening 2M Bids**

There are a few choices for these bids.   Intermediate major 2’s are a great treatment at teams, however we have limited 5cM openings, so this may be taking a burden out of our 1M bids that isn’t really there, we can take out the 1M with 3M rebids and play them here.   Instead, we choose to play these as the std 5M \+ 5m weak 2 suiters.  2M shows the suit bid and an undisclosed 5cm.   

* 2NT/2♥ is forcing for one round with 5+ spades.   This is clearly better, and consistent with our kickback Feature ask.       
* 2M+1 is the strong asking bid, response is to bid 3m with a minimum, 3M with a maximum and the corresponding minor,  except 2NT shows Hearts and Diamonds and a strong hand.  
  * Next step that isn’t one of the suits or 3NT is shortness asking, response structure identical to the 1M jump-shift sequence:  
    * Step 1 \= lower shortness, relay asks whether it’s a stiff or void, responses are stiff, void with 5M, void with 6M  
    * Step 2 \= both short (11, 10, 01, 00\)   
    * Step 3-5 \= higher shortness.     
  * The next step free step is 6 Card RKC  
  * 4M+1 if available is RKC for the major   
* 3m/4m/5♣ is a pass/pull bid.   Over 3♣, 3♦ is the pull bid, however over 3♦ the pull is back to the major if the suit is clubs.   .  
* 3♥/2♠ is invitational.  Opener should generally raise to 4♥ with 2 card support unless its a minimum.  

If the opponents bid, minor suit bids are still pass/pull, jumps re fit showing with the major, and Qbid is the only strong bid,   

# **​4​ Namyats 4m bids** 

An opening 4m shows a 4-4.5 loser hand, and 7+ in the corresponding major.   It generally shows a stand alone suit x AKQJxxxx x AJx, for 4♣ or KQJxxxxx x  \-- Axxx for 4♦ are examples.

When the rules allow, it also applies in these situations:

* 1NT-4m, these are technically not South African transfers since opener has the chance to bid 4M-1.    
* As an overcall of their 1-level opening.   If their opening is natural and not the minor bid it is disallowed under GCC.   Hence over a natural 1♦ opening, 4♣ is not Namyats (presumably preemptive) but 4♦ is.     
* As an overcall of their weak 2, or multi 2♦.  See defense to weak 2\.   
* As a response to 1♣ , this promises 5-5.5 losers..   
* As a response to 1♦  this promises 6-6.5 losers.  This means  1♦-4♠ should be a hand like QJxxxxxx xx xx x,   

## **​4.1​ Responses**

With 3 clear cover cards such as the king of trump and two side aces, responder should just bid the slam.     
4M-1 is a shape asking bid.   Let us assume M=♥. Then after 4♦:

* 4♥ shows one singleton. 4♠ is RKC while 4NT asks for the suit (cheaper/lower)  
* 4♠ shows two short suits.  4NT asks for the long suit (cheaper/lower)  
* 4NT+ shows a void without another short suit (cheaper/lower)  

4M is signoff.    
4M+1 is RKC.  The Namyats bidder does not show a void here, and the RKC bidder should be well aware that voids are plausible.   

4M+2+ asks for control in a particular suit (cheaper/lower) 

* Step 1 is no second round (two quick losers), relay (not 5M) asks for third round control.    
* Step 2 is second round control (King or stiff), relay asks for which. Having shortness is the lower step, having the Honor is higher.     
* Step 3 is first round control (Ace or void), relay asks for which.    
* Step 4 is both first and second round, AK or stiff A

5M is a trump quality ask.   Opener passes with an expected 2 losers or more opposite a void. Steps show 1 loser opposite a stiff, 1 loser opposite a void, solid suit, etc.   

**Interference**  
Over an opening Namyats, we are not in a forcing pass situation, responder is generally captain (doubles are penalty).   The only forcing pass situations are when the Namyats responder has opened 1m or 1NT,   Redouble is generally to play, Pass of a bd lower than 4M acts like 4M-1.   

**3NT Opener**   
3NT is a hand that would have opened with a 4m preempt  Responder bids 4m,5♣ as pass or pull.   4M is to play.   In competition, bidding the minor is pass/pull.  

# **​5​ Slam Bidding Situations**

We play Kickback RKC and Kickback Non-Serious 3M+1.  Cuebids in our system are mixed A/K cuebids.  If you skip a suit  knowing you are going to bid past game, say bid 4♦ then 5♣, then you do show first round control of the first suit and second round control of the second suit.   Cue-bidding and Exclusionary are exceptions to one of our general rules, *we do not play cheaper-lower.*  Instead 4NT (and 3NT if it applies)  are cuebids of the kickback suit, and cuebids of other suits are really the suit bid.  This reduces ambiguity in some sequences.    In minor suit situations, 4NT is often to play however.   

## **​5.1​ Non-serious (Frivolous) 3M+1 and Serious 3M**

3M+1 non-serious only applies when we have a 9-card major fit or a 4-4 major fit with a splinter.   Here 3M+1 is a mild slam try,  and cuebidding is a serious slam try,   3NT if the suit is hearts is a spade cuebid, and a serious slam try,   If partner makes a splinter bid that is exactly 3♠, then 3NT is still a mild slam try, and cuebids are serious.  

In addition we play serious 3M when a GF has been bid and an 8+ card fit has been found already.   Then 3M typically shows strong slam interest and usually promises an extra trump.   We can have situations where serious 3M and non-serious 3M+1 both apply, or one or neither.  

## **​5.2​ 1430 Kickback RKC**

When a suit is agreed, generally 4Suit \+ 1 is Roman Keycard Blackwood.  Agreement also means when one hand has shown a self-sufficient or 7+ suit.   We may also list situations where RKC applies without explicit agreement, but this does not weaken the general understanding that a suit has to be agreed,   One such situation is when a player has already denied 4+ spades and bids 4♠ over a heart bid.   RKC still applies after we have started cuebidding,   

Responses are:

* Step 1 is 1 or 4 Keycards.    
* Step 2 is 0 or 3 Keycards  
* Step 3 is 2 or 5 Keycards without the Queen of Trump  
* Step  4 is 2 or 5 Keycards with the Queen of Trump   
* Step 5 is 2 Keycards with a void (We use Martelsby’s structure, relay \= which suit?)  
* Step 6+ is 1 or 3 Keycards with a void (cheaper/lower)  

After RKC in a minor, a bid of 4NT is generally to play.   If you have a known void, don’t show it again.   If you have a suit which is a known splinter (stiff/void) then just show your keycards, opener knows you can be void.    

### **​5.2.1​ Queen Ask and Specific Kings**

The cheapest relay bid after 14/30 response that’s not 4NT or 5 of the suit asks for the Q of trump.  

* 5Suit always denies.     
* The cheapest response otherwise (could be 5S-1)  shows the Q and no side Kings.  Bids beyond this are specific Q asks (cheaper/lower).     
* Higher steps show specific Kings, cheaper/lower.  Show the lowest King first with two, partner can ask for higher kings

.     
5S-1 over the 14 response or 5S+2 over 03 asks for the Q or J of trump or any exta length.   

The cheapest bid that isn’t signoff or a Q ask (this will usually be 5S+1)  asks for kings,  Response is to show kings cheaper/lower or bid 6S without any kings.  The RKC bidder can then ask for a specific higher king.   6S+1 shows all three kings.

**Interference**   
If there is up to 3 step interference, we play something similar to ROPI/DOPI.  

* Pass \= 14, X is a Q ask  
* XX \= 30, relay is a Q ask.    
* Next step \= 25 w/o Q unless it’s 3 level interference (then it shows 2\)  
* Step+1  \= 25 w/Q.  

Over higher interference, we play DEPO, X=even number of Keycards, P=Odd number.

## **​5.3​ Exclusionary RKC**

In order for this to apply, the bid has to be a jump bid and 4S+1 has to be RKC.  And of course the bidder needs to be able to be void in the suit bid.   The responses are for Keycards outside the suit:

* Step 1 \= 03  
* Step 2 \= 14  
* Step 3 \= 2 

If they interfere, we play DOPI and DEPO.  

## **​5.4​ Six Keycard RKC**

If we have a fit in two suits or a designation in the notes where it is 6 Keycard RKC:

* Step 1 \= 14  
* Step 2 \= 03  
* Step 3 \= 25 w/o Q  
* Step 4 \= 25 w/ 1Q  
* Step 5+ \= 52 w/ 2Q (5,6,7,8 shows side K’s) 

Whenever there is a Q ask, the first step denies a Q, the second step shows 1, the third step shows 2\.

## **​5.5​ 5NT Bids** 

If we have an agreed suit, a jump to 5Suit \+1 is (Kickback) Grand Slam Force, asks for two of the top 3, responses are:

* Step 1 \= none of the top 3  
* Step 2 \= Queen   
* Step 3 \= A/K no extra length   
* Step 4 \= A/K extra length   
* Step 5+ \= two of the 3, suits in addition to suit bid are cues (for 7NT).  

If we do not have an agreed suit, 5NT is Pick a Slam.   This will often be in a high level competitive auction.  We should try not to confuse this with GSF in spades.   

Also in asking situations where there is captaincy,  and a 6 level bid may be an asking bid (in 1♦ opening situations)  we play 5NT as a convenient slam signoff--it’s a realy to 6♣ and then further bids are signoff. This is more I’m Pickin a Slam.        

## **​5.6​ Keycard Asking After Preempts**

A 4♣ response to a preempt either immediately, *which is alertable*, or after a feature-asking sequence, in which case it is explicitly *not* alertable) asks for keycards; responses are 0, 1 w/o, 1 with, 2 w/o, 2 with.  EXCEPTION: if 4♣ is the cheapest relay after a feature response, then 4♣ is the shortness-asking relay and the next higher non-signoff is keycard.

Over a 3♣ pre-emptive opening, 3♣-4♣ is a non-forcing raise, while 3♣-4♦ is simply Kickback RKC.

After a 4♦ response showing 0 keycards, the first step asks for the Q: 

* Step 1 shows no Q  
* Step 2 shows the Q but shows lack of trump quality This is subjective, for a vul preempt if QJxxxx is a minimum, this should be step 2\.  
* Step 3+ shows good trump quality and perhaos a feature, 

After other responses, the cheapest relay asks for trump quality (usually the J and 6+), where the first step denies trump quality, and the next steps show good trump.  

# **​6​ Position Considerations**

Our system notes are by default openings in 1st seat, or if they are defensive methods they assume our opponents are opening 1st seat.   In this section we discuss changes to the notes in other positions.   

## **6.1 Actions in Second Seat**

We don’t have any explicit system changes, as a tactical considerations our openings particularly our preempts are purer--we are less inclined to make them because of one flaw, particularly when 1st seat opens light.   

## **6.2 Third seat considerations**

The major change is when NV, 3rd seat we play 1NT=8-13 balanced, often on a 5 card major especially toward the lower end of the range (we will choose this in favor of passing).  We are not allowed to play any artificial bids over this so all bids are signoff.   This means a 1♣ opener can be 14-17 balanced NV.   

Other than that, preempts are more wide ranging and can be offshape.  Opening 1M can be on a 4cM, especially if it’s lead directing--AKJx and out is fine (but we don’t open 1M with fewer than 8 HCP).    .   

For our other constructive openings 1♣, 2♣, we shade by at most a point.  1♦ is still 17+ and 3 controls is still GF.   

If second seat opens,  we can choose to devalue some of the bottom of our ranges for strong actions for example power doubles over one or two level bids, or the strong 2-suiter.  Hence our overcalls and preempts may be more wide ranging as the minimums drop and the maximums increase.    

## **6.3 Fourth Seat Considerations**

As opener, obviously we can pass out. Our 2-level openings as intermediate strength with the same shape meanings.   This eases memory of the system.   

* 2♣ is now 13-17, or 12 with 3 spades.     
* 2♦ is either 14-17 with a 6cM (a hand that would open 1M and rebid 3M)  or 17-20 4441\. Responses are the same.    
* 2M is 5M \+ 5m but 14-17-, short of jump-shift strength.  It’s a hand that wants to open 1M and rebid 2.5 of the minor.     
* In GCC events 2♦\-2♠ are 14-17 constructive 2’s.  
* 2NT is strong only for the minors, in this case responder can signoff in 3m  
* 3m is a hand that would open 1♣ and bid 3m. 3M is forcing showing 5+.     
* 3M is a good 7+ major, probably short of a Namyats bid  
* 3NT is gambling.    
* 4m is still Namyats  
* 4M is to play, short of Namyats.           

In balancing seat:

* Over weak 2’s and other preempts, our balancing actions are standard.   We don’t play X as a power double, it’s simply a balancing takeout.   We do not play Rubensohl over balancing doubles.    
* Over 1 bids, we still play the Roman jumps and 2-suited Cuebid, and strong 2-suited 2NT.   X is takeout and 1NT is 14-17.   

In direct seat after both players have bid:

* We don’t Balance in Direct Seat after a raise.   A double shows a good hand usually opening values with shape, or a strong hand.  
* After (1A)-(1B):  
  * The main point again is that we don’t compete without a good hand.  
  * 1NT is 15-17 balanced, usually with stopper in the suit bid.    
  * X is a sandwich NT  
  * 2A is a natural suit bid.     

## **​6.4 Passed Hands** 

### **6.4.1 Advancing**

We don’t play transfer advances anymore after a 1/1 Overcall.  Responder cannot have a absolutely forcing hand.   1NT is now natural, 7-9 points,   suit bids are natural,  cue bid is a 3 card LR, 2NT is a 4 card limit raise.   

After a 2/1 overcall, cuebidding always shows support, LR+.  Double shows a raise with Qxx at least (This is either Rosenkrantz or Reverse Rosenkrantz).

Over 1M we play Drury, and Kaplan inversion is off, we describe our 3rd seat 1M response structure.   In 4th seat replace “submin” by 12-13, “min” by 14-15 and “max” by 16-17.   

* 1♠ is 4+ spades, no inversion.  It’s semi-forcing for one round, opener should really raise with 4, unless he has the   
* 1NT is still semi-forcing, partner passes on a subminimum, responder will not have a 3 card limit raise.     
* 2♣ is Drury, shows 3 card support and a LR+, says nothing about clubs  
  * 2M is a submin (compared to 1st seat) opener. Or a min 4th seat opener  
  * 2♦ is a min opener with only 4cM,  says nothing about diamonds

  * 2NT \= Maximum with 4cM, 15-17  
  * Jumps to new suits are natural game tries

* 3♣ shows 6+ clubs and is constructive, stronger than 1NT then 3♣.  
* 2M+1 is still a mini-splinter   
* 3M-1 is still a constructive raise   
* 2NT/1♥ and 3♦/1♠ are LR without shortness  
* All higher bids retain their value we can certainly have GF splinters like QJxxx/x/AKxx/xxx. 

In competition, we play support doubles/redoubles up to and including 2M.   

Over 1NT 11+-14, we play 2♦ natural and signoff.   

Over 2♣, we do not play our 2♥ puppet,  2M is natural and 9+ but can be passed.  2NT is 10-11, 3m is constructive, 8-10.

Over 4th Seat:

* 1NT is a more distributional takeout, implies singleton in the suit and at least 4cM  
* Roman Jumps  and 2 suiters in MC tend to show 4 in the major since 2M not bid  
* Jump bids that were intermediate are now preemptive.   
* 1NT after (1A)-(1B) is again sandwich, showing shape in the two suits (46 ish).

# **​7​ Miscellaneous Competitive Bidding Situations**

## **​7.1​ Rubensohl-style situations**

The Rubensohl-style system applies to interference over 1NT also applies in the following situations:

* (weak2)-X power double  
* when we double a weak NT and they run  
* when we redouble after our weak NT and their runout passes back to responder, e.g. 1NT-(X)-XX-(2♠); P-(P)-? or 1NT-(X)-XX-(P); P-(2A)-?  
* when we open a strong 1♦ and they interfere at the 2-level  
* we make an O.S. power double and they run to the 2-level (whether in opener’s suit or a new suit).  
* When we rebid 1NT showing a balanced hand and they bid

The general rule here is when partner has shown a strong or balanced hand or both, and doubles are at least optional.   

* 2suit is natural and signoff.   
* 2NT is a puppet to 3♣. After the puppet, bidding a suit below the opponent’s (or passing 3♣) is signoff.   Cue bidding after the puppet is forcing with clubs.  Bidding a suit higher than the opponent’s suit after a puppet is weaker than a transfer.    
* Other suits at the 3 level are transfers, invitational value or better.  If partner has an accept, he does not accept the transfer.      
* A transfer into the opponent’s suit is Stayman.  Partner should show a 4 card major if he has one.   Otherwise he should bid 3NT with a stopper or 3 of their suit if he needs help.  Over a 2♣ bid, 2NT is Stayman.   We have signoffs at the 2 level in all other suits, so we don’t need the 2NT lebensohl signoff.    
* 3♠:  
  * if neither partner has bid NT yet, 3♠ is a transfer to play 3NT  
  * If partner has already bid NT, 3♠ asks for a half stopper  
* 3NT directly *shows* a stopper.    So the puppet bid (slow) asks for a stopper.


## **​7.2​ OS Decisions** 

* We play Drury Transfer Advances by an unpassed hand .  2A-1 is a 3 card LR+, 2M-1 is the 4 card LR+  
* 2NT is always a strong 2 suiter.    
* 3♣/1M \= minors  
* 3♦/1♠ \= 55 reds  
* Jump cue is a strong 3-suiter.    
* 4m is Namyats whenever it’s allowed (as a triple jump cue, over artificial 1♦ (Precision) etc. 

In balancing seat, 1NT is 15-17 balanced, it’s reopening could be 14\.  By a passed hand, it’s still sandwich NT, but more distributional than X.   If a multi 2M was available, it probably shows 46 in the two suits.  2NT over a 2/1 is still sandwich.   

## **7.2 Forcing Pass-Double Inversion** 

When a forcing pass situation occurs,  we play Pass-Double inversion, that is:

* X shows a willingness to compete.   If trump is not set, it’s similar to a negative/responsive double.     
* Pass initially shows a willingness to defend.   Partner will generally X with a hand that would normally pass a penalty double.    
* An immediate bid is a hand that can’t stand a penalty pass from partner.   
* Pass and pull is stronger than immediate bidding.    

The advantages of this bid is that the player in immediate seat has three ways to compete--X, bidding or passing and pulling,

 

## **7.3 Support Doubles**

We play support doubles up to and including 2M in these situations

* As responder  a 3rd or 4th seat 1M opening   
* As opener after a 1♣ opening and a 1M response (in comp or not).     
* As opener after a 1♦ opening and a 1M response showing 4+  
* As advancer after a 1/1 overcall. 


# **​8​ Defense to Various Openings**

## **​8.1​ Strong 1NT**

We play Woolsey, this is pretty much directly from Kit:

* X shows 5+ in a minor, 4cM.   2♣ is Pass/Correct, 2♦ asks for the major, 2M, 3m, 3M are natural, 3M is invitational. 2NT asks for further description of the minor--3m shows a minimum with the minor, 3M shows a maximum with the minor.  4m is Namyats. With 18+ you can start with X and rebid 2NT.    
* 2♣ shows both majors, usually 54/45+,  2♦ is pick a major 2M is to play.  3 level suit bids are to play, the only forward going bid is 2NT-- in response 3m is a minimum with preference for the corresponding major, 3M is a max.  
* 2♦ shows one major suit.   2M is pass/pull.  3m is to play, 2NT again is asking/invitational, 3m shows a max with the major, 3M shows a min  
* 2M shows 5+ in that major and 4+ in a minor.  2NT asks for the minor.  2♠ , 3m is to play.   
* 2NT is minors.    

If they play transfers:

* Immediate Qbid is takeout.     
* Pass and then X after signoff is less shape   
* X is lead directing  
* 2NT is minors.

## **​8.2​ Weak 1NT**

A weak NT is defined as a bottom range of 13 or weaker.  

In direct seat:

* Double shows 15+, it’s just another power double.   If responder flees to the 2-level then Rubensohl is on for our side, doubles are penalty.  If they pass 1NT, we play 2NT as Stayman, 3 level bids are Rubensohl.    
* 2♣ shows both majors.  We use the same response structure as above except 3M is invi	tational, 2NT is the strong forward going bid.  3m is non-forcing.     
* Other 2-level overcalls are natural 5+ and constructive.  Raising and 2NT are invitational, new suits are forcing for one round.   If the overcall gets doubled, redouble is to play.     
* 2NT shows clubs. Bidding 3♣ is weak, other bids are GF.   
* 3-level bids are Intermediate Jumps, except for 3♣ which is just an overacall, 13-17 with a 6 card suit.   Double and bid a suit with most 18’s.    
* 4m is Namyats, 4M is to play.  

By a passed hand, we revert to our Strong NT system.   If they play transfers we play the system above.   

If responder signs off in 2M, we treat it as a weak 2M with X as a power double, see below.   

## **​8.3​ Weak 2’s**

Over the opponents’ 2-level pre-emptive openings, we play power doubles showing roughly a strong NT in strength, usually balanced.  2NT is for takeout, generally shows 2- in the suit bid and 3+ in the other suits.   Generally cue bids are Michael’s style, while skipping to the 4m level is first Namyats, and when that is impossible it shows 6+ minor, 4 in a major. We play the same system over a weak NT and signoff in 2M.   

Over 2♠:

* X shows 15+  
* 2NT takeout   
* 3♣, 3♦, 3♥ are natural overcalls  
* 3♠ shows 5+ hearts and 5+ in a minor  
* 3NT to play   
* 4♣ is Namyats for hearts  
* 4♦ is 6+ diamonds and 4 hearts  
* 4NT is minors

Over 2♥:

* X shows 15+   
* 2♠, 3♣, 3♦ are natural  
* 2NT is takeout  
* 3♥ is 5+ spades and 5 in a minor   
* 3♠ shows 6+, intermediate (15-18),     
* 4♣ is 6+ clubs, 4 spades  
* 4♦ is Namyats for spades  
* 4♥ shows a strong hand with 4 spades and 6 diamonds  
* 4NT is minors  

Over 2♦:

* X shows 15+  
* 2NT is takeout  
* 2M, 3♣ is natural  
* 3♦ is 55 in the major   
* 3M is intermediate   
* 4m is Namyats for the corresponding major.   
* 4M is to play, weaker than the Namyats hand

Over a simple overcall, new suits are forcing by an unpassed hand, and still generally forcing by a passed hand.    

## **​8.4​ Strong Club or Strong Diamond or Strong 2♣**

We play the following starting at 1NT+, but the structure repeats at each level 

* NT shows a one suiter in a minor.  Any minor suit bid is pass/pull, and major suit bid is non-forcing and to play. 2NT is the only invite in the minor  
* x♦ shows a one suiter in a major.  Similarly any major is pass/pull any minor suit bid is non-forcing, although x♦ may be passed xNT is invitational 

The following bids are two suiters with the suit bid and either Diamonds or the suit higher.  Hence:

* x♣ \= Clubs \+ Diamonds or Hearts  
* x♥ \= Hearts \+ Diamonds or Spades  
* x♠ \= Spades \+ Clubs or Diamonds 

In response to the two suiters,   bidding one of the two possible other suits is Pass/Pull--pulling is to the cheaper of the two suits.  Bidding the 4th suit is competitive, appropriate length for the bid.   This applies to 3NT and game level bids also.   

The one level bids are still natural, possibly on 4 and lead directing.    Double of 1♣ or 1♦ is Mathe showing both majors.   Obviously the appropriate level of preempt is chosen depending on vul, etc.   

We play the same system over a strong  2♣, except we do not have the one level bids.   Hence X shows Clubs \+ red suit.   

Against Precision, Over 1♣\-1♦ we play the same defense, with X=majors and our conventional one/two suiters starting at 1NT.  Over 1♣-1NT we play the same defense starting with X=one suited in a minor.   Over 1♣\-1M we play Roman jumps.    

## **​8.5​ Unassuming or Polish Club**

Over a 1♣ 10+ opening that may not show clubs, we just make our usual opening bid, with X substituting for 1♣.  An exception is that we don’t play 1NT showing 8-13 NV.   

## **​8.6​ Namyats and Mini-Variants**

In defense to a 4m opening showing Namyats in the corresponding major, we play:

* Double is takeout.   It may be light, for business you can double 4♠  
* Suits are natural   
* Cuebid of 4M is Michael’s-like, 4NT asks for the minor.  
* 4NT is minors 

We have the same structure over a 2m showing a strong hand in the corresponding major, or 2om showing a strong hand in the non-corresponding major. 

* X is takeout, again may be competititve.   
* Cuebid of 2M is Micahels, 2NT asks for the minor.   
* 2NT is minors  
* New Suit bids are natural, but may be weak.    
* 4om is Namyats for the other major.    
* 4m shows 6+ in the minor and 4 in the other major.   
* 3NT is a minor suit preempt

## **​8.7​ Flannery** 

Over 2♦ Flannery, we play 2♥ is takeout, 2♠ is natural (bearing in mind RHO’s distribution), X=14-16 balanced, 2NT=17-19 balanced.  We treat the X as a Rubensohl situation (with hearts as the enemy suit).

Over 2♥ Flannery we drop the 14-16 double.

## **​8.8​ 2NT opening showing the minors**

3♣ is strong takeout (around 15+), 3♦ is competitive takeout.

## **​8.9​ Mini-Roman, other 2-level openings etc.**

In general where it would make sense the X=14-16, 2NT=17-19 is a meta-agreement.

# **​9​ Defensive Carding**

We play upside-down count and attitude, right-side-up suit preference.  Discards are usually upside-down attitude.

vs. suits we lead A from AK (except when it makes sense not to), 3rd/5th meaning low  from odd, as high as possible from even.  We are not shy about underleading aces and kings even against suits.  

vs. NT we lead 4th best, top or 2nd best of nothing (including possibly top or 2nd from xxxx depending on the opening leader’s own attitude in that suit).

Honor leads are otherwise standard.

We do not play any conventional echo when declarer runs a suit at NT or draws trumps.

We play obvious shift principles with a small ‘o’.   That is we do not have a suit designated to be the obvious shift each time.  However when a suit is clear to both partners as the obvious shift, an honor discard suggests the non-obvious shift.  We also will signal suit preference on the opening lead at times.    

# **​10​ GCC Switches From MidChart**

## **​10.1​ Weak 2 Bids** 

In GCC events we no longer play Multi.  Hence our 2-level bids are weak 2’s.   We play Kickback Feature where the cheapest relay asks for a feature (cheaper/lower).   After the feature ask, the cheapest relay which isn’t signoff asks for shortness, 4♣ is our Preempt Keycard. Jumps are fit-showing.  

If the opponents make a takeout double, responder’s non-jump new suits are non-forcing and tend to deny a fit.  Jump shifts are fit-showing as if without the interference.  Responder’s doubles are for penalty; redoubling a takeout double shows a strong hand (XX followed by a new suit is forcing one round below game).

If the opponents bid, jumps are fit showing, including 4♣.   New suits are possibly lead directing and non-forcing.   Qbid is the only strong bid.  


## **​10.3​ 1♠/1♥ Non Inversion** 

We play 1♠ as forcing with 4+ Spades, and a forcing NT denying 4 Spades.   All responses are similar except we have 1♥-1♠ \-1NT.showing 12-14.   In this case we use 2♣ as artificial and asks for 3 card spade support or 6 hearts.
