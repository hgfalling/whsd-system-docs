### ‚Äč1.4.2‚Äč¬†Forcing Pass/Double Inversion 

Whenever we are in a forcing pass, generally after a GF bid (not a bid to game) has been made--we invert the meanings of pass a double.  This principle also applies at a lower level when a GF has been established such as over 1‚ô¶.   That means that double tends to be takeout and pass suggests penalty.  

* Pass in direct seat generally suggests a hand that would have doubled or a hand that is confident to pull.   Here we have two ways to pull--passing and pulling is stronger and invites further competition,     
* Double in direct seat is a hand that would have passed.   Partner can immediately pass the double for penalty or pull with a hand he would have pulled opposite a pass.  
* An immediate pull means the same as it usually does except it takes out a strong way to pull.   Example, a pull to 5M immedicately is mostly competitive and not a real invite to 6M.   

At the lower levels when a fit hasn‚Äôt been found, like after 1D-(?)-1NT, a direct double acts more like takeout, while a pass is optional/penalty.   This actually clarifies some auctions.  

In balancing seat after a Pass, Double shows a hand that would accept a penalty double.   After a double, balancer makes the same bid he would over Pass.
