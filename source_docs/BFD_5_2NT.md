# **BFD: 2NT opening**

The 2NT opening shows 0-10 or very strong (3.5-loser hand), 5-5+ in the minors.  Bill and Spencer use the following response structure:

* pass to play 2NT (not uncommon\!)  
* 3m to play  
  * raises by opener (with or without competition) merely show extra distribution and not necessarily a strong hand  
  * 3M shows shortness in that major  
  * 4M is kickback for the corresponding minor  
* 3M natural and forcing  
* 3NT to play  
* 4m pre-emptive (4M by opener is kickback)  
* 4M kickback for corresponding minor  
* 4NT, 5NT to play in opener's better minor at the next higher levelt  
* 6NT to play

Opener shows the strong hand type by making an unusual followup bid or double, **EXCEPT** that raising responder merely shows extra distribution, and not necessarily a strong hand.

If the opponents interfere, bids of 4M are signoff, except a 4M cue-bid is 6-keycard RKC.

Doubles are for penalty.
