# **BFD: Introduction/Overview**

BFD was conceived by William Chen and Spencer Sun with the express intent of creating a strong 1♦ system within the confines of the [ACBL General Convention Chart](http://www.acbl.org/assets/documents/play/Convention-Chart.pdf).  Accordingly, it does not require written prepared defenses, although based on the [ACBL Alert Procedures](http://www.acbl.org/play/alertprocedures.html) it probably still requires a pre-alert per section 3, “SYSTEMS THAT MAY BE FUNDAMENTALLY UNFAMILIAR TO THE OPPONENTS.”  

The 5-second summary: is “Precision with the meanings of the 1♣ and 1♦ openings exchanged.”  Making 1♣ the catchall permits the use of a 1♦ waiting response, allowing opener to bid 4-card majors naturally and in general leading to clearer sequences when opener has a 4M+5m or single-suited minor hand.  We also especially like the 2♣ opener.

## **Summary of BFD opening bids**

| 1♣ | 11-16 unbalanced or 15-17 balanced, denies a 5-card major |
| :---: | :---- |
| 1♦ | Strong opening, 17+ (18+ if balanced or 5cM) |
| 1M | Limited to 11-17, but otherwise any 5-card major system\* (Bill and Spencer use Lawrence-style 2/1) |
| 1NT | 11\+\-14 weak NT framework |
| 2♣ | 11-16, no 4-card major, at least 4-5/5-4 in the minors (could be 5-5, 4-6, 7-4, etc.) |
| 2♦, 2M | Bill and Spencer play these as weak 2s, but other typical meanings would also work (unloading some 1♣-opening shapes with mini-Roman might not be the worst idea) |
| 2NT | 5-5 or better in the minors, weak (0-10) or strong (3-loser hand) |

Higher opening bids are left to partnership agreement.

\*Because 1M openings are limited, there is less pressure on responder to bid 1NT on crappy hands (although opener may still choose to open a borderline 2-suited hand 1M instead of 1♦).  Speaking of which, one arguably minor flaw in our opening structure is that since 1♣ denies a 5cM, two suited opening hands with a 5+ major and a longer minor must be opened 1M.  Fortunately this is rare.
