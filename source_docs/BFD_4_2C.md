# **BFD: 2♣ Opening**

This is possibly our favorite part of BFD.  The 2♣ opening shows 11-16 HCP with 4-5/5-4 or better in the minors, no 4-card major.

\*In *Positive Declarer Play* by Reese & Pottage, problem \#29 on page 65 features the auction 2NT-6NT accompanied by the commentary “Most of the modern partnerships would take seven or eight rounds of bidding to reach this obvious contract.”  2♣-3NT sometimes feels the same way albeit on a smaller scale.

## **Updated Response Structure (August 2015\)**

2♦ is preference to diamonds.   In the Relay and ask notes it could be an asking bid.  

2♥ is now a puppet to 2♠, after which

* 2NT, 3m natural and invitational  
* 3M is game forcing with the corresponding minor   
* 3NT is 55 in the majors, GF but may be passed   
* 4♣ is 6-keycard RKC  
* 4♦ is RKC for clubs  
* 4♥ is RKC for diamonds  
* 4♠ asks for length in minors. This bid or 5♠ also applies later in other auctions:  
  * 4NT equal length, usually 55 but could be 66  
  * 5m, longer in that minor

Opener only breaks the puppet if he has a bid over a 2♠ signoff.   

2♠ is a one round force with 5+ spades,  
2NT is now a one-round force with 5+  hearts.  Over both 2♠ and 2NT:

* 2NT where possible is a min with stopper in other major (hearts).    
* 3m is the longer minor. 3♣ with 55  
* 3M is a minimum raise of the major (can be honor doubleton)  
* 3oM is a good raise in the major, principle of criss-cross majors, *after this bid note 3NT is a club cue if we play Gerber.*    
* 3NT shows a maximum with a stopper in the other major,   
* 4m is a max with support, shortness in oM, and strength fragment in the suit bid.  

3NT, 3m, 4m, 5m directly are all to play.  

3M is stopper asking in the other major.  Responses are:

* 3NT shows a stopper in the other major.    Other bids show no stopper and speak to the length of the other major which is important according to our simulations. 

* 3♠ shows 3 small hearts.  Responder may bid 3NT also with 3 small.    
* 4m shows the longer minor without a stopper, denies shortness in the other major.  Responder may decide to signoff here, this is the only case.     
* 4♥ shows a singleton in the other major.  4♠ is the minor length ask, 4NT is invitational--opener declines the invite with 5♣.  This is an exception to the 4NT to play rule, responder can’t be playing here,       
* 4♠ shows a void in the other major.  This should practically be forcing to slam.  4NT is RKC (5 keycard because it’s a known void).  5♠ is length-asking in the minors.     
* 4NT shows a first or second round stopper and a max.   5♥ asks which with 5♠ showing 2nd round control (the King).   5NT directly or after 5♥ is a transfer to 6♦ so that opener can play the contract (logical since he has a king).    

4M is to play, we have forcing bids in each major if we are more interested in slam.    
4NT is choose a minor

## **Interference**

Minor suit bids by responder are competitive; 4NT asks for better minor; doubles are penalty-oriented by responder and negative by opener (something like 0355 or 3055 shape).

* Over (X), XX is card-showing, other bids including the puppet are the same  
* Over (2♦) showing majors: 2M/3M is invitational/forcing with corresponding minor, 3m competitive  
* Over (2M): 2♣-(2♥)-2♠ retains its original meaning; **2♣-(2♠)-2NT still shows hearts**.  Cue-bid of 3M is stopper-asking, while 3oM is slammish in one of the minors. 4M are still RKC for corresponding minor.  
* Over (2NT): X is penalty-oriented, 3M natural/forcing, 3NT \= pick a minor (either signoff or slammish)  
* Over (3m): regardless of what 3m shows, X is penalty-oriented, 3M and 4M are natural  
* Over (3M): 3♠ natural/forcing, 2♣-(3♥)-4♠ is still RKC for diamonds, but 2♣-(3♠)-4♥ is to play.  
* Over (3NT): X is penalty, other bids natural
