# **BFD: 1♦ Opening**

1♦ is our strong forcing openings.  In a more fully-developed version of the system (which we no longer use due to infrequent play) opener is generally captain and uses relays/asking bids to extract information from responder and then place the contract.  Since opener knows when the side lacks controls for slam, responder should generally respect opener's signoff wishes (especially jumps to 3NT) unless he has something extraordinary like an undisclosed void.  
Basic responses:

* 1♥: 0-4\- HCP (but respond 1♠ with an ace)  
* 1♠: 5+ HCP or an ace (if you want to get cute, 5-18), but at most 2 controls (A=2, K=1)  
* 1NT, 2♣, 2♦, 2NT: 3-6 controls, GF.  **NOTE:** 2♦ by a passed hand is pre-emptive  
* 2M, 3-level bids: pre-emptive, 3-6\- HCP  
* Higher suit bids still show weak hands that would otherwise be suitable for a 1♥ response, but have some expectation of making  
* 3NT..7NT: 7-11 controls

### **Exact odd number of controls**

If responder has shown exactly 3 controls, he can have 0, 1, or 2 keycards, with 1 being the expected number.  We order the responses expected, low, high.  Thus, if spades are trumps and responder has 3 controls, the responses to 4NT are:

* 5♣: 1 keycard, either an ace and a side-suit king (SSK), or the trump king and two SSK; see below for continuations  
* 5♦: 0 keycards, i.e. 3 SSK  
* 5♥+: 2 keycards, i.e. any ace plus king of trumps (no SSK); skip directly into the trump-ask (see continuations below)

The important pattern to note is that with the expected response, you can have 1 or 2 SSK depending on whether your one keycard is an ace or the king of trumps.  With the low response you have all 3 SSK and with the high response you have the trump king but no SSK at all.

The 5 control situation is exactly like 3 controls except adding an ace, so the step responses show 2, 1, and 3 keycards. and so on for 7 controls.

### **Exact even number of controls and 0-2.**

If responder has shown exactly 4 controls, he can have 1 or 2 keycards. Assuming spades are trumps, the responses to 4NT keycard-asking are:

* 5♣: 1 keycard (the low number) \-- either an ace and two side kings; or all four kings. See below for continuations.  
* 5♦+: 2 keycards (the high number) \-- two aces and no kings; or an ace (in any suit), the king of trumps, and a side king; skip directly into the trump-ask (see continuations below).

The pattern here is the low keycard response includes 2 or 3 SSK while the high keycard response shows 1 or 2 SSK.  6 controls is the same but with an additional ace, and so on.

### **Keycard after 1♦-1♥ or 1♦-1♠ and equivalent situations**

When responder has made a 1♥ response or its equivalent, he denies an ace, so the most keycards he could have is one (i.e. the trump king).  If responder has shown 0-2 controls, he can have at most an ace or two kings, but in either case no more than one keycard.  Therefore we treat these situations as “exact even number of controls” and 0/1 keycards, with the slight difference that in the 1♥ case, if responder has 1 keycard he cannot have any side-suit king (or queen, for that matter).  In the 0-2 controls case, responder can have an ace and 0 SSK, the trump king and 0-1 SSK, or no keycards and 0-2 SSK.

### **Continuations**

Over a 5♣ response, 5♦ asks for trump quality, while skipping to 5♥ asks specifically for one of QJ of trump or extra length (if the original response was 1♥ or 1♠, show a side feature, i.e. J or Q as appropriate). 5NT is specific Q’s or Kings depending on if partner side king situation is determined.**NOTE:** if a minor suit is trumps, both 4NT and 5m are signoffs, not asking bids.

### **Responses to  trump-asking**

If the first response is 4♠ or 5♠ then that bid denies 2+ extra or the Q.  NT+ is the Q with 0, 1, 2, etc extra. Otherwise: 

* Step 0 is no extra length or honors (Q or J)  
* Step 1 is J or one extra (relay asks which--J, \+1 length)  
* Step 2 is the Q (0 or 1 extra),  (no, J, \+1 length)   
* Step 3+ is 2+ extra, etc

If only 5♥ is available it’s a normal Q ask.   Partner bids 5♠ without the Q or two extra, 5NT with specifically the Q, and 6♣+ as two extra.  In this case 6♣+ specifically asks for the Jack of Trump or one extra.   

### **Responses to 5NT King or Queen-asking**

If you have an exact number of controls, in the case of 0 or 3 side kings, partner will be cognizant of this fact and 5NT asks for side Q’s.  This is because our side should have all the keycards, and thus opener will know if responder is counting the king of trump as a keycard.   Hence 5NT is a K-ask if responder has one or two side kings.   Responder should just bid the suit in which he has king if he has one and the suit in which he doesn’t have a king if he has two.  This also applies in the case of 0-2 controls if you have shown one keycard. 

In the *specific case*  of 0-2 controls where you have shown 0 keycards,  you could actually have two side kings that opener does not know about   In this particular case show your lowest king.    

*We do not play cheaper/lower here, you generally just bid suit in which you have the K or Q (or don’t have in the case of two side kings)*.   In all of these auctions, responder is free to bid on if he has a source of tricks that opener doesn’t quite know about, etc.   

6♣ asks for the Jack of Trump or one extra if there was no way to ask for it before.  Otherwise all six level bids ask for 3rd round control of the suit bid (or other extras). Again, don’t play cheaper/lower here, because it’s easy to get confused in these high level auctions, 6♦ asks for diamonds, etc.
