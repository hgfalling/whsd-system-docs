# WHSD: 1M Opening — Competitive Auctions

*Consolidated from WHSD §4.1 and discussion notes — April 2026*

---

## Overarching Principle

**All constructive system is off the moment the opponents intervene.** The 2♣ semi-puppet and 2♦ GF are dead after any opponent action over 1M. What remains is competitive/natural bidding with a few carefully chosen strength-showing calls.

Rationale: opener is tightly defined (8–11), so responder rarely has invitational or GF hands opposite an opening-bid-ish interference. Unlike standard bidding, we don't need to cater for "partner might have a 19 count" — opener's range is narrow enough that responder can simply jump to game when confident, or compete naturally.

**Responder with a fit and a decent hand can jump to game ("could be serious" jump).** This is not a slam try — just confident-to-make bidding with values in the limit-raise-plus-a-little range. Slam investigation in these auctions is rare enough not to systematize.

**4♣ remains RKC Gerber** for opener's suit in all competitive auctions below.

## Part 1: Over a Takeout Double

**1M – (X) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **XX** | 14+ HCP, implies non-fit, willingness to defend/penalize |
| **1NT** | 9–11 HCP, 2– of opener's major, natural |
| **2♣ / 2♦** | Natural, non-forcing |
| **2 of other major** | Natural, 5+, non-forcing |
| **2M** | Standard raise, non-forcing |
| **2NT** | Limit raise or better in opener's major (the only strength-showing fit bid) |
| **3m / 3oM** | Preemptive (new-suit jump) |
| **3M** | Preemptive raise |
| **4M** | To play — wide-ranging, from preempt through "confident to make" |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber for opener's major |

**Notes:**
- XX is strictly non-fit. Any fit-showing action goes through 2NT or a direct raise.
- There is no forcing bid below game other than XX. Constructive sequences are gone.
- Preemptive double raises are assumed in 2026 — this is not an agreement, it's hygiene.

## Part 2: Over a Natural Overcall

**1M – (overcall) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **X** | Support double equivalent / strength-showing, optional. See notes below. |
| **1NT** *(if available)* | Natural, stopper-showing, non-forcing |
| **New suit at 2-level** | Natural, non-forcing, tends to deny 3-card support |
| **New suit at 3-level** | Natural, forcing one round |
| **Cue of overcaller's suit** | 3+ card support, limit raise or better |
| **Jump cue** | Stopper ask — "bid 3NT with a stopper, otherwise do something intelligent" |
| **2NT** | The other two unbid suits, 5–5ish, constructive values (see notes) |
| **Jump raise of opener's major** | Preemptive |
| **Jump to game** | To play, wide-ranging |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |

**Notes:**

- **2♣ and 2♦ are natural and non-forcing** when available. All puppet/GF machinery is gone.
- **No fit-showing jumps.** A 3-level new suit is natural and forcing, not fit-showing. The only practical way for opener to decline is to rebid the original suit.
- **2NT (unbid two-suiter)** should have at least some values. Partner will typically show his better unbid suit or rebid his own, so it's fine to plan to pass a simple preference. But bidding on after a simple preference pushes 3NT — so continuations after 2NT are **game forcing**.
- **Cue = limit raise or better, 3+ support.** With a 4-card cue raise, opener with only 4 in the major should strive to explore (3NT or another suit) rather than jumping to 4M — responder can always correct back to a 4–4 fit. This is the tricky 4-card-major spot that 5-card-major adherents avoided.
- **No Rubensohl here.** Rubensohl applies in other contexts (2M openings, 2♦ openings, 1NT auctions), not over 1M + overcall.
- **1-level overcall (e.g., 1♥ – (1♠) – ?)**: 2♣ is **natural non-forcing**, not the semi-puppet. The constructive system is off regardless of how much space the overcall consumed.

## Part 3: Over 1NT Overcall or Power Double

**Setup:** "Power double" refers to the strong takeout double in the Inverted Raptor / Shuster overcall structure (15+ or 16+ depending on whether opponents play standard or constructive). Along with a natural 1NT overcall (15–18ish), these are the auctions where **the opponent has announced 14+ HCP**. Treated identically.

**1M – (1NT) – ?** and **1M – (X power) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **X** *(over 1NT)* / **XX** *(over power X)* | Support double: exactly 3-card support, good 8+ HCP, theoretically unlimited |
| **1NT** *(if available — over power X only)* | Natural, competitive, 2– of the major |
| **2♣ / 2♦ / 2oM** | Natural, non-forcing, tends to deny 3-card support |
| **2M** | 4-card support, competitive (bad-13-or-less) |
| **2NT** | Limit raise or better |
| **3-level new suit** | Natural, forcing (rare — tends to deny support) |
| **Jump raise of M** | Preemptive |
| **4M** | To play |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |

**Notes on "competitive" strength:**

Responder has up to ~13 HCP within "competitive" here. Opponents have claimed 14+, so our side has roughly 17–23 combined — often short of game values even with a fit. "Competitive" does NOT mean 6–9 as in standard; a bad 13 opposite 8–11 is a competitive raise, not an invitation.

**Support doubles:** The defining feature of this scenario. Because opponents have shown ~opening values, 3-card vs. 4-card support matters more than strength differentiation at the low level. The support double/redouble splits raises by **length**, reserving 2NT for genuinely strong hands with a fit.

**Why the support double requires a good 8+:** If we support-doubled on 6 HCP opposite 8–11, it would be too easy for opponents to penalize us at the 2-level. We need enough to survive being doubled.

**Defensive doubles later:** All later doubles in these auctions are optional/card-showing. We don't play trap passes — too hard to arrange opposite a limited opener.

**1NT by responder (available only over power X):** Purely competitive. We're not trying to reach 3NT with 17–23 and no fit — just buying the contract cheaply.

## Part 4: Over Two-Suited and Artificial Bids

The structure depends on how much we know about the opponents' suits.

### 4A: Both Opponent Suits Known

Applies when opponents make a bid showing two specific suits: Unusual 2NT, Roman jump overcalls, top-and-bottom cue bids, etc.

**1M – (two-suiter showing suits X and Y) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Cheapest cue** (of X or Y) | Support for opener's M, limit raise or better |
| **Other cue** (of X or Y) | Game force, 5+ in the unbid suit |
| **Double** | No fit, defensive values, willingness to penalize — optional (see notes) |
| **Direct raise (2M/3M)** | Competitive support — fit but sub-limit-raise values |
| **New suit** | Non-forcing, constructive. Opener can raise to game with the right hand |
| **2NT** *(if available)* | Invitational, stoppers in both opponent suits |
| **Jump to 4M** | To play — wide-ranging, same "could be serious" style as Parts 1–3 |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber for opener's major |

#### Example: 1♥ – (2NT showing minors) – ?

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **3♣** (cheapest cue) | Heart support, limit raise or better |
| **3♦** (other cue) | GF, 5+ spades |
| **X** | No heart fit, defensive values, happy to penalize if partner agrees |
| **3♥** | Competitive heart raise |
| **3♠** | Non-forcing, constructive, natural — spades but not GF values |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |
| **4♥** | To play |

Note: 2NT is consumed by the opponent's bid, so the invitational-with-stoppers option is unavailable here.

#### Example: 1♠ – (3♣ Roman showing ♣+♥) – ?

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **3♥** (cheapest cue) | Spade support, limit raise or better |
| **3♣** | *This is their bid — not available* |
| **X** | No spade fit, defensive values, optional |
| **3♦** | Non-forcing, constructive, natural diamonds |
| **3♠** | Competitive spade raise |
| **3NT** | To play |
| **4♣** (other cue) | GF, 5+ diamonds |
| **4♠** | To play |

Note: Here the cheapest cue (3♥) is below the raise (3♠), so competitive raises are straightforward. The "other cue" (4♣) happens to coincide with the Gerber level — in context, it's the GF-with-diamonds bid, not Gerber. Gerber applies when you've already agreed a trump suit, not when you're still showing a new suit.

### 4B: One Opponent Suit Known

Applies when opponents show one specific suit plus an unspecified suit: e.g., Michaels (1♥–(2♥) = spades + a minor).

- **Cue of the known suit** = support for opener's M, limit raise or better
- **Everything else** follows Part 2 (natural overcall) rules: direct raise = competitive, new suits NF at 2-level, double = values/optional

#### Example: 1♥ – (2♥ Michaels, showing ♠ + minor) – ?

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **2♠** (cue of known suit) | Heart support, limit raise or better |
| **2NT** | Invitational, stoppers |
| **3♣ / 3♦** | Natural, non-forcing, constructive |
| **3♥** | Competitive heart raise |
| **X** | Values, no fit, optional — per Part 2 |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |
| **4♥** | To play |

The unknown minor doesn't change the structure — you can't cue a suit you don't know. If the minor becomes known later (e.g., advancer bids 3♦), treat subsequent doubles as optional/card-showing.

### 4C: Artificial Overcalls at the 1-Level

Transfer overcalls, Mathe-style doubles showing both majors, and similar artificial 1-level actions are too rare to systematize. Use judgment. The general principle — "constructive system is off, bid naturally" — applies.

### Notes

**Double is optional, not strict penalty.** Consistent with Parts 1–3: we don't play any doubles that command partner to pass. "Defensive values, willingness to penalize" means you'd be happy defending but partner can pull with extreme shape. Knowing both opponent suits makes penalty more viable than usual, but the mechanism is the same optional double used everywhere else.

**Why new suits are non-forcing.** Opposite an 8–11 opener, game is rarely on when opponents have a two-suiter eating our space. A responder with a genuine GF and a single long suit can use the higher cue bid (GF with 5+ in the unbid suit). Since that route exists, forcing new suits would add nothing — it would just make it dangerous to compete with a constructive but non-GF hand.

**2NT = invitational with stoppers** is only meaningful when 2NT isn't consumed by the opponent's bid. After Unusual 2NT, it's unavailable. After Michaels or Roman jumps at the 3-level, 2NT may or may not be available depending on the auction.

## General Principles Referenced

- **Constructive system off over any opponent action** (1M-specific, but likely generalizes — flag for general_principles.md)
- **"Could be serious" jumps to game**: wide-ranging, preempt through confident-to-make, not a slam try
- **No trap passes**: all later doubles are optional/card-showing
- **4♣ = RKC Gerber** throughout
- **Opener's range is narrow (8–11)**, so responder as captain can place the contract with confidence once a fit is known
