# WHSD: 1M Opening — Direct Responses (Uncontested)

*Consolidated from WHSD §4 and discussion notes — April 2026*

---

## 1M Opening Summary

**8–11 HCP** (or Rule of 17 in 1st seat), **4+ cards** in the major. May be canape (longer side suit). 3rd/4th seat reverts to standard.

Opener is limited → **responder is captain**.

**Key difference from 1♥:** No 1♠ response exists. 2♥ is natural and non-forcing (5+ hearts, weak — wants to play 2♥). 2NT is the mini-splinter, not a natural invite — the way to invite without shortness over 1♠ is through the 2♣ puppet.

## §1. 1♠ over 1♥ — Opener's Rebids

1♠ is responder's weakest spade action. It could be quite weak. Opener's rebids:

| Opener's Bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **Pass** | Minimum, no spade fit |
| **1NT** | Minimum, balanced |
| **2♣ / 2♦** | Canape — longer minor (or 5-5) |
| **2♥** | 6+ hearts |
| **2♠** | 4+ spade support |

After opener's rebid, all continuations are **natural judgment** — no special agreements.

**Four tiers for spade hands over 1♥** (documented in detail in the *2♣/2♦ Sequences* doc):

| Bid | Strength | Spades |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 1♠ direct | Weak / competitive | 4+ |
| 2NT direct | Invitational | Exactly 4 |
| 1♥–2♣–2♦–2♠ | Invitational | 5+ |
| 2♦ (GF) | Game forcing | Any |

---

## §2. 1NT Response (~7–13)

A wide-range catch-all for hands that are too weak to invite (via 2♣ puppet) and have no fit to raise, no long suit to drop in, and no 4+ spades (over 1♥).

The range looks broad but makes sense: opener is only 8–11, so even 13 opposite 11 is just 24 HCP — well short of game. Invitational values start around 14+.

This is typically a dead-end bid. Opener passes most of the time. With extreme shape (6+ in the major, or a strong canape), opener can rebid naturally.

---

## §3. 2M Raise

Wide-ranging, **not particularly constructive** — the primary purpose is competitive (making the opponents bid one level higher).

- **3+ trumps.** Raising with exactly 3 is fine when the hand has shortness or other features that make those trumps likely to be useful, but don't routinely raise with 3 since opener might have only 4.
- **Strength: wide.** Could be quite weak. The raise is more about shape and obstruction than constructive values.
- **Opener rarely continues** — game after a simple raise opposite an 8–11 opener is uncommon. But with an exceptional hand (e.g., ♠x ♥KQJxxx ♦Ax ♣xxxx), opener can bid on.

---

## §4. 2NT over 1♥ (Invitational, Exactly 4♠)

Shows invitational values and exactly 4 spades. This is the second tier of the spade-showing scheme (see §1 above). Opener evaluates and accepts or declines.

This bid does not exist over 1♠ — that slot is the mini-splinter.

---

## §5. Mini-Splinter (2♠ over 1♥ / 2NT over 1♠)

Shows **4+ trump support, invitational or better, and guarantees shortness** somewhere. This is the shortness counterpart to the 3♦ limit raise (which denies shortness).

### Opener's Relay = Shortness Ask

Opener's next step is a relay asking where the shortness is. Since shortness is guaranteed, the "no shortness" step from the standard protocol is **skipped** — saving a level.

**After 1♥–2♠–2NT** (shortness ask, three candidate suits: ♣ ♦ ♠):

| Step | Bid | Meaning |
|------|-----|---------|
| 1 | 3♣ | Short **clubs** → relay 3♦ asks: next step (3♥) = singleton, skip (3♠) = void |
| 2 | 3♦ | Short **diamonds** → relay 3♥ asks: next step (3♠) = singleton, skip (3NT) = void |
| 3 | 3♥ | Singleton **spades** |
| 4 | 3♠ | Void **spades** |

**After 1♠–2NT–3♣** (shortness ask, three candidate suits: ♣ ♦ ♥):

| Step | Bid | Meaning |
|------|-----|---------|
| 1 | 3♦ | Short **clubs** → relay 3♥ asks: next step (3♠) = singleton, skip (3NT) = void |
| 2 | 3♥ | Short **diamonds** → relay 3♠ asks: next step (3NT) = singleton, skip (4♣) = void |
| 3 | 3♠ | Singleton **hearts** |
| 4 | 3NT | Void **hearts** |

### After Shortness Is Known

**Responder drives.** Opener's range is 8–11 with limited side controls, so responder evaluates the fit and decides:

- **Place the contract** (3M, 4M, 3NT) with purely invitational hands
- **Cue-bid** a side control with slam interest
- **Bid RKC (4♣ Gerber)** or kickback with strong slam hands

Opener can cooperate with cue-bids but is rarely the one driving to slam.

---

## §6. 2♥ over 1♠ (Natural, Non-Forcing)

5+ hearts, weak hand that wants to play 2♥. Opener passes or corrects (e.g., back to 2♠ with a minimum and short hearts).

This bid exists because a 1♠ opener cannot have exactly 4 hearts — so there is no major fit to find, and responder just wants to land in a playable spot.

---

## §7. 3♣ Signoff

6+ clubs, weak. **Drop-dead** — opener passes almost always.

Exception: opener can pull with a singleton club and extreme shape (e.g., 1=6=3=3 with good hearts), but this is rare. The default is to respect the signoff.

---

## §8. 3♦ Limit Raise or Better (No Shortness)

**4–5 trump support, invitational or better, no shortness.** Hands with shortness use the mini-splinter (§5) instead — this is the clean partition.

### Continuations

Opener bids naturally:

| Opener's Bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **3M** | Decline — minimum, not enough to accept |
| **4M** | Accept — wants to be in game |
| **Other** | Natural — extras, possible slam interest |

**Responder's follow-up depends on strength:**
- **Invitational only:** Accepts opener's decision. Passes 3M or 4M.
- **GF+:** Continues over opener's 3M sign-off (bids game, cue-bids, or bids RKC).

---

## §9. 3M Preemptive Raise

To play. Opener passes most of the time, but is "allowed to extend with distribution" — e.g., with a 6-card suit and a side source of tricks, opener can bid 4M.

---

## §10. 3NT / 4M — To Play

Responder has evaluated the hand and placed the contract. No further discussion.

---

## §11. 4♣ — RKC Gerber

0-3-1-4 structure for opener's major. See general principles for the full RKC/Gerber protocol.
