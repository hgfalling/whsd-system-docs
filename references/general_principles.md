# WHSD General Principles

These principles apply across multiple sections of the system. Individual section documents reference these rather than restating them.

## After 1M opening bids

### Opener's New Suits After 1M

When opener bids a new suit after opening 1M, it shows canape (longer side suit) or 5-5+. This is shown opportunistically — opener doesn't force the canape into the auction if it doesn't fit naturally. If the hand is minimum and responder signs off, opener can let the canape go.

### Puppet Break Inference

After 1M–2♣–2♦ (puppet accepted), any unexpected action by opener means "bad canape, rejecting the suggested strain." A *good* canape or extras would have broken the puppet. So opener's new suits after the puppet is accepted show minimum hands with distributional reasons not to play the suggested contract.

## Shortness Asks

See the standalone document: **WHSD Shortness Ask Protocol**

Two protocols:
- **Two-Suiter Shortness Ask**: When askee has shown 9+ cards in two known suits
- **Standard Shortness Ask**: When no two-suiter has been described

## Shortness Showing (When Showing Directly, Not Via Ask)

When a player shows shortness directly (not in response to an asking sequence), the rule is **cheaper = lower**.

## Forcing vs. Non-Forcing Defaults

After 1M (limited opening), responder is captain:
- New suits by responder below 2M are non-forcing
- 2♣ semi-puppet and 2♦ GF are the only forcing mechanisms
- After an invitational sequence, opener can accept/decline/correct — no new forcing bids
