# WHSD: 2M Opening (1st and 2nd Seat)

---

## Opening Requirements

2♥ and 2♠ show intermediate single-suited hands:

- **11–15 HCP**
- **5+ cards** in the bid major
- **At most semi-balanced**: 5422 with values concentrated in the long suits, 6322, or a hand with a shortness (singleton or void)

Pure 5332 hands always open 1NT (11–14) or 1♣ (15–17).

With a good 15 to a bad 17 and an unbalanced hand with 5 of a major, opener bids 1♣ instead and jump-rebids the major. With a good 17, open 1♦ instead. 

---

## §1 — Uncontested Responses

Responses use a modified **Lebensohl/Rubensohl** structure. The key features:

- **2NT** is a semi-puppet to 3♣, used as a signoff mechanism for weak hands
- **Transfers** at the 3-level show invitational-or-better values
- Transferring into our suit is an invitational raise.
- Completing a transfer = declining the invitation (minimum)
- Not completing = accepting on strength, game force established

### Over 2♥

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **2♠** | 5+ spades, inv+, no heart fit (see §2) |
| **2NT** | Semi-puppet to 3♣ (see §3) |
| **3♣** | Usually 6+ diamonds (could be 5 in a pinch), inv+ |
| **3♦** | Invitational raise with 3+ card support |
| **3♥** | Simple raise, 9 or fewer points. Mostly just competitive. |
| **3♠** | Spade or club splinter (see below) |
| **3NT,4♥/4♠** | To play |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |
| **4♦** | Splinter in diamonds |

### Over 2♠

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **2NT** | Semi-puppet to 3♣ (see §3) |
| **3♣** | Usually 6+ diamonds (could be 5 in a pinch), inv+ |
| **3♦** | 5+ hearts, inv+ |
| **3♥** | Invitational raise with 3+ card support |
| **3♠** | Simple raise, 9 or fewer points. Mostly just competitive. |
| **3NT,4♥/4♠** | To play |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |
| **4♦** | Splinter in diamonds |

No unspecified splinter is available over 2♠.
Over 2♥, responder's 3♠ is a spade or club splinter. 3NT by opener asks, 4♣=clubs, 4♦=spades.

## §2 — The 2♠ Response to 2♥

2♠ over 2♥ shows 5+ spades, invitational or better, with no heart fit. This bid exists because responder cannot transfer into spades — 3♥ is a sinple raise.

### Opener Accepts (2NT)

2NT is artificial and establishes a game-forcing auction. Responder then clarifies:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **3♣** | 4+ clubs |
| **3♦** | 4+ diamonds |
| **3♥** | 2-card heart tolerance |
| **3♠** | 6th spade |
| **3NT** | Slam interest opposite a maximum |

Responder's priorities should be to show a sixth spade, then two hearts, then a minor. Opener proceeds naturally from there.

### Opener Declines (3-level bid)

Opener bids at the 3-level, showing shape:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **3♣** | 4-card club side suit |
| **3♦** | 4-card diamond side suit |
| **3♥** | 6th heart |
| **3♠** | 3-card spade support |

Opener shows spade support, then a sixth heart, then a minor.
Responder can still have a game-forcing hand and proceeds naturally.

## §3 — The 2NT Semi-Puppet

2NT asks opener to bid 3♣. Responder then passes (club signoff), bids 3♦ (diamond signoff), or — over 2♠ — bids 3♥ (heart signoff). After the semi-puppet, any bids below 3M are signoff. Bidding opener's major after the puppet is a constructive raise, around 9+-11 hcp. This allows 3M directly to be weaker.

**Opener can break the puppet** at the top of range with a good reason. Opener bids shape naturally:

- 3M = 7-card suit
- New suit = 5-5+

---

## §4 — Summary of Ways to Raise

After 2M, responder can raise in the following ways:
- 3M directly: mostly just competitive.
- 2NT first, then 3M: weak invitation.
- 3(M-1): transferring into our suit = inv+ support
- 4M: To play

## §5 — Rubensohl Transfer Follow-ups

After a transfer at the 3-level:

### Opener Declines (Completes the Transfer)

Completing the transfer shows a minimum. Responder can still bid on with a game-forcing hand — the transfer was "inv+" so responder may have been GF all along.

### Opener Accepts

Opener has three options when accepting:

| Action | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Bid game in responder's suit** | Accepting in responder's suit |
| **3NT** | To play |
| **Rebid own major** | Natural, forcing — looking for a 6-2 fit |

Space is tight at this level. The primary goals when accepting are finding 6-2 major fits or landing in 3NT.

---

## §6 — Interference Over 2M

When opponents act over a 2M opening:

- **Double** is optional
- **New suits** are forcing
- **Cuebids** show support
- **Fit-showing jumps** apply
- **3M** is a constructive raise — the lower end of a limit raise, frequently raised to 4M.
