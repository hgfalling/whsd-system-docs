# WHSD: 1M Opening (1st and 2nd Seat)

---

## 1M Opening Summary

The 1M opening is the centerpiece of the WHSD system. It is the single departure that transforms what BFD was — a slightly unusual strong diamond system with weak NT — into something genuinely off the beaten path. Opening 8–11 HCP hands with a four-card major is the most disruptive break from standard methods and allows us to open the bidding much more often than our opponents.

1M in 1st or 2nd seat shows:
- **8–11 HCP** (or Rule of 17: HCP + combined length of two longest suits >= 17).
- 4+ cards in the major.
- Could be canape, with a longer other suit (including the other major).
- With both majors, open the shorter major.
- With the same length, open 1♥.
- Note that a 1♠ opening denies exactly four hearts. With 4-4 in the majors, we would open 1♥ and with longer spades, we would open the shorter four-card heart suit.

Opener is limited and narrow → **responder is generally captain**.

**Document organization:** Parts 1–3 cover uncontested auctions (direct responses, the 2♣ semi-puppet, and the 2♦ artificial game force). Part 4 covers competitive auctions — when the opponents act over our 1M opening.

3rd/4th seat reverts to a much more standard approach — see **DOCUMENT NAME HERE** for that structure.

# Part 1: Simple Responses

## §1. 1♠ over 1♥ — Opener's Rebids

1♠ is responder's weakest spade action. It could be quite weak. Often in this situation opponents will act, as we have likely a lot less of the strength. Our bidding has gotten us into the auction and the opponents are somewhat disrupted, there's no reason to overcompete here.

Opener's rebids:

| Opener's Bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **Pass**/**1NT** | Natural, thinks this is the best action |
| **2♣ / 2♦** | Canape — longer minor (or 5-5) |
| **2♥** | 6+ hearts |
| **2♠** | 4+ spade support — would typically always make this bid |

After opener's rebid, all continuations are **natural judgment** — no special agreements.

**Four tiers for spade hands over 1♥:**

| Bid | Strength | Spades |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 1♠ direct | Weak / competitive | 4+ |
| 2NT direct | Invitational | Exactly 4 |
| 1♥–2♣–2♦–2♠ | Invitational | 5+ |
| 2♦ (GF) | Game forcing | Any |

---

## §2. 1NT Response (~7–13)

A wide-range catch-all for hands that are too weak to invite (via 2♣ puppet) and have no fit to raise, no long suit to drop in, and no 4+ spades (over 1♥).

The range looks broad but makes sense: opener is only 8–11, so even 13 opposite 11 is just 24 HCP — well short of game. Invitational values start around 14+.

This is often the last bid of the auction for our side. Opener frequently passes. With shape (6+ in the major, or a canape that looks bad for NT), opener can rebid naturally.

---

## §3. 2M Raise

Wide-ranging, **not particularly constructive** — the primary purpose is competitive (making the opponents bid one level higher).

- **3+ trumps.** Raising with exactly 3 is fine when the hand has shortness or other features that make those trumps likely to be useful, but we don't routinely raise with bad 3-trump hands since opener might have only 4.
- **Strength: wide.** Could be quite weak. The raise is more about shape and obstruction than constructive values.
- **Opener rarely continues** — game after a simple raise opposite an 8–11 opener is uncommon. But with an exceptional hand (e.g., ♠x ♥KQJxxx ♦Ax ♣xxxx), opener can bid on.

---

## §4. 2NT over 1♥ (Invitational, Exactly 4♠)

Shows invitational values and exactly 4 spades. This is the second tier of the spade-showing scheme (see §1 above). Opener evaluates and accepts or declines. There is no corresponding bid over 1♠, as opener cannot have four hearts when opening 1♠.

---

## §5. Mini-Splinter (2♠ over 1♥ / 2NT over 1♠)

Shows **4+ trump support, invitational or better, and guarantees shortness** somewhere. This is the shortness counterpart to the 3♦ limit raise (which denies shortness).

### Opener's Relay = Shortness Ask

Often after this bid, opener will just kind of know where to place the contract. With a marginal accept or a very good hand, he might want to know where responder's shortness is. Opener's next step is a relay using the shortness guaranteed ask from the shortness ask document. Notice that sometimes the shortness ask responses might take opener past 3M; he should be ready to accept invitations from hands that would do this.

Example: 
**After 1♥–2♠–2NT** (shortness ask, three candidate suits: ♣ ♦ ♠):

| Step | Bid | Meaning |
|------|-----|---------|
| 1 | 3♣ | Short **clubs** → relay 3♦ asks: next step (3♥) = singleton, skip (3♠) = void |
| 2 | 3♦ | Short **diamonds** → relay 3♥ asks: next step (3♠) = singleton, skip (3NT) = void |
| 3 | 3♥ | Singleton **spades** |
| 4 | 3♠ | Void **spades** |

### After Shortness Is Known

Opener should know whether he's accepting an invite, and whether he has a hand useful for slam in the case of responder having extras. So bidding the major at the 3 or 4 level shows min acceptance/decline, after which opener would need significant extras to go on. Or opener can cue bid now to cooperate with a responder with extras to investigate slam. After this responder is generally the captain, opener has shown just about everything.

---

## §6. 2♥ over 1♠ (Natural, Non-Forcing)

5+ hearts, weak hand that wants to play 2♥. Opener passes or corrects (e.g., back to 2♠ with a minimum and short hearts) or could bid a canape if that seems indicated.

---

## §7. 3♣ Signoff

6+ clubs, weak. **Drop-dead** — opener passes almost always.

Exception: opener can pull with a singleton club and extreme shape (e.g., 3631 with good hearts), but this is rare.

---

## §8. 3♦ Limit Raise or Better (No Shortness)

**4–5 trump support, invitational or better, no shortness.** Hands with shortness use the mini-splinter (§5) instead.

### Continuations

Opener bids naturally:

| Opener's Bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **3M** | Decline — minimum, not enough to accept |
| **4M** | Accept — wants to be in game |
| **Other** | Natural — extras, possible slam interest |

**Responder's follow-up depends on strength:**
- **Invitational only:** Accepts opener's decision. Passes 3M or 4M.
- **GF+:** Continues over opener's 3M sign-off (bids game, cue-bids, or bids RKC).

---

## §9. 3M Preemptive Raise

To play. Opener passes most of the time, but is allowed to extend the preempt with appropriate distribution as a pre-sacrifice or to put pressure on the opponents. 

---

## §10. 3NT / 4M — To Play

Responder has evaluated the hand and placed the contract. This includes 4♥/1♠ and 4♠/1♥.

---

## §11. 4♣ — RKC Gerber

0314 structure for opener's major. See general principles for the full RKC/Gerber protocol.

# Part 2: The 2♣ Semi-Puppet

2♣ by responder is a semi-puppet to 2♦. It promises **invitational values** unless responder intends to pass 2♦ (diamond signoff). Opener either accepts the puppet (bids 2♦) or "breaks" with a hand worth describing.

## §12. Opener's Rebid After 1M–2♣

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **2♦** | Default — nothing special to show |
| **2M** | 6+ in opened major, upper range |
| **2oM** *(see note)* | Canape — other major is longer (or equal if hearts over 1♠) |
| **2NT** | Balanced maximum (~11 HCP), could have 5M or 4♠ if opened 1♥ |
| **3m** | Canape — 5+ in the minor, equal or longer than major, upper range |

**Note on 2oM:** After **1♠–2♣**, opener always breaks to show 2♥ with 5+ hearts (any strength), because 2♥ is cheap and stays below 2♠. After **1♥–2♣**, opener bids 2♠ only with upper range, since it bypasses 2♥.

**Key negative inferences after 2♦ acceptance:**
- After 1♠–2♣–2♦: opener does NOT have a heart canape (would have bid 2♥)
- After 1♥–2♣–2♦: opener could still have a bad canape of any type
- After either: opener is not a balanced maximum (would have bid 2NT), does not have 6+ in the major with extras (would have bid 2M), does not have a strong minor canape (would have bid 3m)

**General principle — opener's new suits after 1M:** A new suit bid by opener (whether as a puppet break or later in the auction) shows canape or 5-5+. It is shown opportunistically, not forced. If the auction doesn't provide an opportunity, opener can let it go.

## §13. After 1♥–2♣–2♦: Responder's Rebid

Every rebid is **invitational and natural**, except pass.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Pass** | Diamond signoff |
| **2♥** | Invite, exactly 3 hearts |
| **2♠** | Invite, 5+ spades |
| **2NT** | Invite, balanced, 0–2 hearts |
| **3♣** | Invite, natural, clubs |
| **3♦** | Invite, natural, diamonds (not a signoff — pass was the diamond signoff) |

3♥ does not exist here — responder has many direct ways to show heart support (2♥ raise, 2♠ mini-splinter, 3♦ limit raise, 3♥ preemptive raise).

**The spade hand tiers over 1♥ (four levels):**

| Bid | Strength | Spades |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 1♠ direct | Weak / competitive | 4+ |
| 2NT direct | Invitational | Exactly 4 |
| 1♥–2♣–2♦–2♠ | Invitational | 5+ |
| 2♦ (GF) | Game forcing | Any |

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Pass** | Diamond signoff |
| **2♥** | Invite, 5+ hearts, natural |
| **2♠** | Invite, exactly 3 spades |
| **2NT** | Invite, balanced, 0–2 spades |
| **3♣** | Invite, natural |
| **3♦** | Invite, natural |

## §15. Opener's Action After Responder's Invitational Rebid

These follow from natural logic and negative inference (anything "interesting" would have been shown as a puppet break). No special agreements needed — general principles apply:

- **Accept** by raising or bidding game
- **Decline** by passing
- **New suit** = bad canape, rejecting the suggested strain. (A good canape would have broken the puppet.)
- **Re-invite** = marginal accept, giving responder a last chance

Example after **1♥–2♣–2♦–2♠** (invite, 5+ spades):

| Opener's bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Pass | Decline, spades is fine |
| 2NT | Natural, accepting the invitation toward NT |
| 3♣/3♦ | Bad canape, doesn't want to play spades |
| 3♥ | 6 hearts, weak (strong 6 would have broken puppet) |
| 3♠ | Re-invite — spade support, marginal accept |
| 3NT | Accept game, reject spades |
| 4♠ | Full accept |

## §16. After 1M–2♣–2NT

Opener has shown a balanced maximum (~11 HCP). Since responder had invitational values, this is essentially a game-forcing auction.

**Responder can:**
- Place the contract directly (3NT, 4M) if they've heard enough
- Bid **3♣ (NMF-style)** to find a major fit

**After 1♥–2♣–2NT–3♣:**

| Opener's bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 3♥ | 5 hearts |
| 3♠ | 4 spades (opened 1♥ with 4-4 majors, balanced max) |
| 3NT | 4 hearts, no 4 spades |

**After 1♠–2♣–2NT–3♣:**

| Opener's bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 3♠ | 5 spades |
| 3NT | 4 spades |

---

## §17. After Opener Breaks the Puppet (Non-2♦ Rebid)

If opener breaks with 2M, 2oM, 2NT, or 3m, responder adjusts accordingly. Key agreement:

- **3♦** by responder = "I had a diamond signoff; wish you hadn't had something to say."

Otherwise, responder evaluates the new information and bids naturally.

### General Principles in 1M–2♣ Auctions

- Responder shows invitational values or a diamond signoff.
- Opener breaks with 4-5 major canapes or canapes in top half strength, and max balanced hands.
- When opener breaks, unless responder bids 3♦ showing a signoff, auctions are GF (if opener bids 2NT), near GF (minor suit canapes), with the exception of heart canapes, where opener could be minimum.

# Part 3: The 2♦ Game Force

2♦ by responder is **artificial and game forcing**.

## §18. Opener's Rebid After 1♥–2♦

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **2♥** | Balanced or semi-balanced — no shortness, no 6-card major |
| **2♠** | Unbalanced, 5+ hearts (either 6+ hearts or has a shortness) |
| **2NT** | Canape — 5+ spades, longer or equal to hearts |
| **3♣** | Canape — 5+ clubs, longer than hearts |
| **3♦** | Canape — 5+ diamonds, longer than hearts |

## §19. Opener's Rebid After 1♠–2♦

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **2♥** | Unbalanced, 5+ spades (either 6+ spades or has a shortness) |
| **2♠** | Balanced or semi-balanced — no shortness, no 6-card major |
| **2NT** | Canape — 5+ hearts, longer or equal to spades |
| **3♣** | Canape — 5+ clubs, longer than spades |
| **3♦** | Canape — 5+ diamonds, longer than spades |

**Structural note:** 2oM (the "other major") always shows an unbalanced hand with 5+ in the opened major. 2NT shows a canape in the other major.

## §20. After the Balanced/Semi-Balanced Rebid

After 1♥–2♦–2♥ or 1♠–2♦–2♠:

Opener has shown a balanced/semi-balanced hand (no shortness, no 6-card major). Could have 5M or a 4-card side suit.

**After these sequences:**

| Responder's bid | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| 2♠ | if available: 5+ spades — asking opener to show spade support or else a side suit |
| 2NT | General ask — show a 5th card in the major, or a 4-card suit, or bid 3NT with 3=4=3=3 exactly |
| 3♣/3♦ | Natural |
| 4♣ | RKC for the opener major |
| 4NT | Quantitative (if 3NT is a viable contract) |

**After the 2NT general ask, opener responds:**
1. Show a 5th card in the major (rebid 3M)
2. Otherwise show a 4-card suit (bid it naturally)
3. Otherwise 3NT (exactly 3M=4=3=3 after 1♥, or 4=3=3=3 after 1♠)

After opener answers, bidding continues naturally, no shortness asks here.

---

## §21. After the Unbalanced Rebid — Shape Ask & Shortness Ask

After 1♥–2♦–2♠ or 1♠–2♦–2♥ (unbalanced, 5+ in opened major):

1♠–2♦–2♥–2♠ sets spades as trumps, and starts the shape ask as below. Opener should bid 2NT with 6+ spades and no side suit instead of 3NT to save space. By implication, if responder bypasses 2♠ to shape ask with 2NT, he denies 3 spades at least.

**Step 1 — 2NT = Shape Ask** (looking for a 4-card side suit)

After **1M–2♦–2oM–2NT:**

| Opener's bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 3♣ | 5 of the major, 4+ clubs |
| 3♦ | 5 of the major, 4+ diamonds |
| 3♥ | 6+ of the major, 4 clubs |
| 3♠ | 6+ of the major, 4 diamonds |
| 3NT | 6+ of the major, no side suit. (except see above) |

---

**Step 2 — Relay = Shortness Ask**

After opener answers the shape ask, the next relay by responder asks for shortness.

**→ See *WHSD_Shortness_Ask_Protocol.md* for the full shortness-asking system.**

Which protocol applies depends on what's been shown:
- If opener has shown a 4-card side suit (e.g., 1♥–2♦–2♠–2NT–3♣ = 5♥ + 4♣, 9 cards known) → **Two-Suiter Shortness Ask**
- If opener has shown only 6+ in the major with no side suit (e.g., 1♥–2♦–2♠–2NT–3NT) → **Standard Shortness Ask**

**Example: 1♥–2♦–2♠–2NT–3♥–3♠ (two-suiter shortness ask)**

Opener has shown 6+ hearts and 4+ clubs. This is the two suiter protocol. 3♠ is the relay. Steps from 3♠:

| Step | Bid | Meaning |
|------|-----|---------|
| 1 | 3NT | Short diamonds, relay, steps 2614, 3604, 2605, 2704 |
| 2 | 4♣ | 1624 shape |
| 3 | 4♦ | Spade void, relay, steps 0634, 0625, 0724 |
| 4 | 4♥ and higher | 1s1d, 1s0d, 0s1d, 0s0d, relay for exact shapes |

### General Principles in 1M–2♦ Auctions

- Opener rebid: 2NT = canape (or 55+) in other major, 3m = canape (or 55)
- 2♦ bidder is generally captain, opener should largely just describe his hand.
- Shortness asks could be Two Suiter or Standard, depending on what opener has promised.

# Part 4: Competitive Auctions

**All constructive system is off when opponents intervene.** The 2♣ semi-puppet and 2♦ GF are dead after any opponent action over 1M. What remains is competitive/natural bidding with a few carefully chosen strength-showing calls.

Rationale: opener is tightly defined (8–11), so responder rarely has invitational or GF hands opposite an opening-bid-ish interference. Unlike standard bidding, we don't need to cater for "partner might have a 19 count" — opener's range is narrow enough that responder can simply jump to game when confident, or compete naturally. Slam is generally remote when opponents interfere.

**Responder's jumps to game are "could be serious".** 
These can be anything from straight preemptive to solidly bidding to make. 

**4♣ remains RKC Gerber** for opener's suit in situations where that is at all reasonable.

**1M – (X) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **1♠** | natural, 4+ spades, non-forcing. |
| **XX** | 14+ HCP, 3 or fewer of opener's major, willingness to defend/penalize |

## §23. Over a Natural Overcall

**1M – (overcall) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **X** | Optional/card-showing, **not a negative double.** If opener bids, responder's bids are forcing as followups up to 2NT. |
| **1NT** | Natural, stopper-showing, non-forcing |
| **New suit at 2-level** | Natural, non-forcing |
| **New suit at 3-level** | Natural, forcing one round |
| **Cue of overcaller's suit** | 3+ card support, limit raise or better |
| **Jump cue** | Stopper ask — "bid 3NT with a stopper, otherwise do something intelligent" |
| **2NT** | The other two unbid suits, 5–5ish, constructive values (see notes) |
| **Jump raise of opener's major** | Preemptive |
| **Jump to game** | To play, wide-ranging |
| **4♣** | RKC Gerber |

**Notes:**

- **2♣ and 2♦ are natural and non-forcing** when available. All puppet/GF machinery is gone.
- **No fit-showing jumps.** A 3-level new suit is natural and forcing, not fit-showing. Auction becomes game-forcing unless opener rebids his original suit. 
- **2NT (unbid two-suiter)** should have at least some values. Partner will typically show his better unbid suit or rebid his own, so it's fine to plan to pass a simple preference. Bidding on after a simple preference is game forcing.
- **Cue = limit raise or better, 3+ support.** With a 4-card cue raise, opener with only 4 in the major should strive to explore (3NT or another suit) rather than jumping to 4M as responder can always correct back to a 4–4 fit. This is the tricky 4-card-major spot that 5-card-major adherents avoided.

## §24. Over 1NT Overcall or Power Double

"Power double" is the Overcall Structure double (15+ and usually balanced). Along with a natural 1NT overcall (15–18ish), these are the auctions where the opponent has announced **14+ HCP**.

**1M – (1NT) – ?** and **1M – (X power) – ?**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **X/XX** | Support: exactly 3-card support, usually some values. Theoretically unlimited in strength; other actions tend to deny 3-card support. |
| **1NT** | Natural, competitive, 2– of the major |
| **2♣/2♦/2oM** | Natural, non-forcing, tends to deny 3-card support |
| **2M** | 4-card support, competitive but no game interest |
| **2NT** | Limit raise or better, 4+ support |
| **3-level new suit** | Natural, forcing (rare — tends to deny support) |
| **Jump raise of M** | Preemptive |
| **4M** | To play, "could be serious" |

**Notes on "competitive" strength:**

Responder has up to ~13 HCP within "competitive" here. Opener is limited so our side has roughly 21–24 combined — often short of game values even with a fit. 

**Support doubles:** The defining feature of this scenario. Because opponents have shown ~opening values, 3-card vs. 4-card support matters more than strength differentiation at the low level. The support double/redouble splits raises by **length**, reserving 2NT for genuinely strong hands with a fit.

**Why the support double usually has values:** If we support-doubled on trash opposite 8–11, it would be too easy for opponents to penalize us at the 2-level. We need enough to survive being doubled.

**Defensive doubles later:** All later doubles in these auctions are optional/card-showing. We don't play trap passes — too hard to arrange opposite a limited opener.

## §26. One Opponent Suit Known (Two-Suited Bids)

Applies when opponents show one specific suit plus an unspecified suit: e.g., Michaels (1♥–(2♥) = spades + a minor).

- **Cue of the known suit** = support for opener's M, limit raise or better
- **Everything else** follows §23 (natural overcall) rules: direct raise = competitive, new suits NF at 2-level, forcing at 3 level, double = values/optional

### Example: 1♥ – (2♥ Michaels, showing ♠ + minor) – ?

| Bid | Meaning |

### General Notes on Two-Suited Auctions

**Doubles are optional, not strict penalty.** Our doubles in competition are typically optional-style, only occasionally strict takeout, and we rely on partner's judgment to pull.  

**2NT = invitational with stoppers** is only meaningful when 2NT isn't consumed by the opponent's bid. After Unusual 2NT, it's unavailable. After Michaels or Roman jumps at the 3-level, 2NT may or may not be available depending on the auction. Also frequently with stoppers in both their suits, we'd be doubling instead.
