# WHSD: 2♦ Opening 

---

## Opening Requirements

- **11–16 HCP**
- **4-5 / 5-4 or better** in the minors (at least 9 minor-suit cards)
- **No 4-card major**

### General Notes: 

- Most invitational sequences go through the 2♥ puppet (exception: responder's major suit bids).
- 2♦-3NT is an extremely common sequence. Hiding information about declarer's shape (like which majors he has four cards in) is worth something in the play.
- 4NT is basically never RKC or Blackwood unless we specifically find a spade fit. It is frequently to play in slam investigation auctions where we want to stop short. It can also be "pick a minor" in auctions where no suit has been agreed.

## §1 — Uncontested Responses

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **2♥** | Puppet to 2♠ (see §2) |
| **2♠** | 5+ spades, one-round force (see §3) |
| **2NT** | 5+ hearts, one-round force (see §3) |
| **3♣/3♦** | To play |
| **3♥/3♠** | Stopper ask in the other major (see §4) |
| **3NT** | To play |
| **4♣/4♦/4♥/4♠/5♣/5♦** | To play |
| **4NT** | Choose a minor |

## §2 — The 2♥ Puppet

2♥ asks opener to bid 2♠. Responder then clarifies:

| Responder's Rebid | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pass** | Signoff in 2♠ |
| **2NT** | Natural, invitational |
| **3♣/3♦** | Natural, invitational |
| **3♥** | GF with clubs (hearts correspond to clubs) |
| **3♠** | GF with diamonds (spades correspond to diamonds) |
| **3NT** | 5-5 majors, GF (may be passed) |
| **4♣** | 6-keycard RKC (see General Principles) |
| **4♦** | RKC for clubs |
| **4♥** | RKC for diamonds |
| **4♠** | Minor length ask |

Minor length ask responses (also applies at 5♠ in other auctions where space is consumed):
- 4NT = equal length (usually 5-5)
- 5m = longer in that minor

**Opener breaks the puppet** only at the top of range with big shape — hands like 1-0-6-6, 2-0-7-4, or a very good hand with spade support (e.g., 3-0-5-5). Opener bids naturally: 3m shows the longer minor, 3♠ shows spade support.

## §3 — Major Suit Responses (2♠ and 2NT)

2♠ shows 5+ spades, one-round force. 2NT shows 5+ hearts, one-round force.

Opener's rebids over both:

| Opener's Rebid | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **2NT** (over 2♠ only) | Minimum with a heart stopper |
| **3♣** | Longer minor is clubs (bid 3♣ with 5-5) |
| **3♦** | Longer minor is diamonds |
| **3M** (responder's suit) | Minimum raise (can be honor doubleton) |
| **3oM** (other major) | Good raise in responder's major |
| **3NT** | Maximum with stopper in the other major |
| **4m** | Max with support for responder's major, shortness in oM, strength fragment in the suit bid |

### After 3M (Minimum Raise)

Opener could have honor doubleton here, so:
- **3NT** is choice of games
- **kickback** is RKC
- **other 4-level suit bids** = cuebids

### After 3oM (Good Raise — Major Fit Found)

Now have a real major suit fit, so: 
- **3NT** = club cue bid
- **4♣** = RKC Gerber
- **other 4-level bids** = cue-bids

## §4 — Stopper Asks (3♥ and 3♠)

3♥ directly over 2♦ asks for a **spade** stopper.
3♠ directly over 2♦ asks for a **heart** stopper.
These imply but do not promise stoppers in the bid suit.

### Opener's Responses

| Rebid | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **3♠** (over 3♥ only) | 3 small in the asked suit (hearts) |
| **3NT** | Has a stopper in the asked suit |
| **4m** | Longer minor, no stopper, denies shortness in the asked suit |
| **4♥** | Singleton in asked suit |
| **4♠** | Void in asked suit |
| **4NT** | First or second round control in asked suit + max |

The "3 small" idea here is that 3NT may be playable without a stopper if we each have 3 small. 

### After Singleton 

- **4♠** = minor length ask
- **4NT** = slam invitational; opener declines with 5♣

### After Void (4♠)

- **4NT** = RKC (5-keycard, excluding known void)
- **5♠** = minor length ask

More or less forcing to slam.

### After Control + Max (4NT)

- **pass** = to play (common)
- **5m** = to play
- **5♥** = asks which control; 5♠ = second round (the king)
- **5NT** (directly or after 5♥) = transfer to 6♦, so opener plays the contract (logical since opener has the king)

## §5 — Interference

The auction is already at a pretty high level here, so there is a lot of judgment and natural bidding. Only a few sequences have specific non-obvious meanings.

General principles: 
- Minor suits bids by responder are competitive
- 4NT asks for better minor
- Doubles are penalty-oriented by responder and takeout-ish (given the constraints on the 2♦ hand) by opener (showing something like 0-3-5-5 or 3-0-5-5 shape).

### Over (X = majors)

XX is card-showing and penalty oriented. Otherwise, systems on. 

### Over (2♥/2♠ natural)

| Bid| Over | Meaning |
|----|------|---------|
| X | both | penalty-oriented |
| 2♠ | 2♥ | 5+ spades, inv+ |
| 2NT | both | natural, invitational, stopper |
| 3♥ | 2♠ | 5+ hearts, inv+ |
| 3m/4m/5m | both | competitive |
| 3M | both | stopper-asking (except 3♥/2♠)|
| 3NT | both | to play |

Here we lose "invitational in minor" bids for both opponent suits, and 2NT invitational with a stopper is probably more valuable than showing hearts at the lower level, so we go with that and punt the heart-stopper ask when the opponents bid spades.

### Over (2NT = balanced with strength)

- X = penalty-oriented
- Minor suit bids: competitive
- 3M = natural, forcing
- 3NT = pick a minor (signoff or slammish)

### Over (3m = corresponding major)

- X = penalty-oriented of their major
- Minor suit bids are competitive.
- 3oM/4oM = natural
- 3NT = to play
