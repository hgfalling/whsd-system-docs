# WHSD: 1♣ Opening (1st and 2nd Seat)

---

## Opening Requirements

**11+ HCP**, bounded above by 1♦ (17+ unbalanced, 18+ balanced) and below by 1M (8–11 with a 4+ card major).

Contains hands in the following categories:

- **Balanced, 15–17 HCP.** Systemically includes balanced hands with a 5-card major.
- **Single major, good 15 to bad 17.** Semi-balanced or unbalanced with 5+M and 3 or fewer cards in the other major — too good for 2M but not good enough for 1♦.
- **Single long minor, 11–16.** 6+ cards in one minor, 3 or fewer in the other.
- **Minor-major, 11–16.** 5+ card minor with a 4-card major, unbalanced.
- **Oddballs, 11+.** 4441, 5440, and any other hands with no other home in the system.

## Uncontested Responses

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Pass** | Rare. Should promise clubs. |
| **1♦** | Artificial waiting bid. Mostly negative inferences: cannot be 8+ with a 4cM, cannot be 8+ balanced. Unlimited. |
| **1♥** | 4+ hearts, 8+ HCP. Bid 1♥ with 4-4 in the majors. With 5-5, bid 1♠ with less than invitational strength, otherwise start with 1♥. |
| **1♠** | 4+ spades, 8+ HCP. With 4-4 majors, bid 1♥ instead. With 5♠–4♥, bid 1♠. |
| **1NT** | 8–10 balanced, denies a 4-card major. |
| **2♣** | Both majors, 5-4+, 6–11 HCP. |
| **2♦/2♥/2♠** | Preemptive, 6+ cards, 3–7 HCP. |
| **2NT** | 11–12 balanced, denies a 4-card major. |
| **3NT** | To play. 13–15 balanced, or any hand that wants to declare 3NT opposite a 1♣ opener. Opener should generally pass even with 17 unless holding a good 6+ minor looking for 6m or 6NT. |
| **3♣–3♠** | Preemptive. |
| **4♣/4♦** | Namyats (long major, 5–5.5 losers). |
| **4NT** | Pick a minor. |

With minor-suit hands below game force, responder bids 1♦ and sorts it out later, or bids NT if practical.

## §2 — 1♣-1♦-? Opener's Rebids

1♦ is a quite nebulous bid, primarily characterized by what it's not: 

- **Responder cannot have 8+ HCP and also a 4-card major suit.**
- Could have a major with weaker strength than that, but also has available 2-level major suit responses with 6+ cards, so usually not a 6-card major unless the hand is really bad.
- Responder didn't respond in NT, which covers balanced hands without a 4-card major from 8-15.
- So responder is usually either 0-7, or unbalanced with any strength with an emphasis on the minors.

---
Opener clarifies hand type:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1M** | 4-card major. Bid 1♥ with both. Unbalanced (or 4225/4252 types). |
| **1NT** | 15–17 balanced. Can contain a 5 card major. |
| **2m** | 6-card minor, denies a 4cM. |
| **2M** | 16–17, 5+M, at most semi-balanced, denies 4+ in other major. |
| **2NT** | 15–17, 6322/7222 shape with a 6+-card minor — no shortness. A 3m bid with a shot at right-siding 3NT. |
| **3m** | 15–17, 6+ card minor with at least a shortness. |
| **3M** | 6+M, ~4.5 losers. Stronger than 2M but short of opening Namyats — perhaps in length or suit quality. |
| **3NT** | Gambling — long running minor you expect to run, plus one outside stopper. |
| **4M** | To play. Stronger hand than Namyats (opener could have bid 4m instead). |

The 2NT vs 3m distinction is **shortness**: 2NT = 6322 (no shortness), 3m = at least a singleton.

## §3 — 1♣-1♦-1M Continuations

Opener has shown a 4-card major and is typically unbalanced with a longer minor (or 4441 shape) (balanced hands would have opened 1NT or rebid 1NT). Responder is unlimited but cannot have 8+ with a 4cM (would have responded 1M directly). 

Responder's hands divide into game-forcing (13+), inv (10+-12), constructive (8-10-) and weak (0-7):  

| Rebid | Strength | Meaning |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Pass** | weak | Bad hand with 3-card support, or very bad with 4. |
| **1♠** | weak | *(After 1♥ only.)* 4+ spades — didn't bid 1♠ directly. |
| **1NT** | weak | Artificial, no support for M. |
| **2m** | constructive | Often 6-card suit, could be 5. |
| **2M/3M/4M** | weak | Preemptive raises with 4 card support. |
| **2oM** | GF | Artificial game force (New Major). See §3.1. |
| **2NT** | inv | Invitational, generally with stopper in oM. |
| **3m** | inv | Good 6-card minor. Invitational. |
| **3oM** | GF | Meckwell — wants partner to declare 3NT. |
| **3NT** | GF | To play. |
| **4m** | GF+ | RKC Gerber, setting m as trumps. |

### §3.1 — GF Hands: New Major Game Force (2oM)

Bidding the **other major** at the 2-level is an artificial game force. Responder can't really have that major, so the focus is finding a NT contract or a minor-suit game/slam. **All game forcing hands go through this bid** (or jump to game/slam sequences).

**Opener's 3rd-round rebids after 2oM:**

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **2NT** | Stopper in oM, says nothing else about the hand. |
| **2♠** | *(After 1♣-1♦-1♠-2♥ only.)* Artificial waiting bid — denies a heart stopper or extra values. |
| **3m** | 5+ minor, denies oM stopper. Does not show extras if no 2♠ waiting bid was available. |
| **3NT** | Extra values (15–16) with a good stopper in oM. |
| **3oM** | Meckwell — wants partner to declare 3NT. |
| **4m** | Extras with a distributional hand (e.g. 4-6 or 4-7). |

**After opener rebids 2NT showing oM stopper:**

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **3NT** | Signoff (minimum). |
| **3m** | Natural, minor-suit game/slam interest. |
| **3M** | 3-card support for opener's major. |
| **3oM** | Both minors. |

## §4 — 1♣-1♦-1NT (15–17 Balanced)

Opener shows 15–17 balanced. Responder cannot have 8+ with a 4cM (would have responded 1M), so we have weaker hands with longer major suits that want to transfer and drop, and unbalanced hands with focus on 3NT or minor games/slams. 

Our solution is to use a **three way puppet(ish) structure** where 2♣, 2♦, and 2♥ show weak hands with both majors, hearts only, and spades only, respectively OR game forcing and slam interested hands with clubs, diamonds, and both minors, respectively. Even though usually the partner of a NT bidder is captain, these GF sequences are designed to help the NT bidder evaluate his hand for minor suit slams. 

### Responder Rebids

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **2♣** | Either 0-5 with 5-4/4-5 or better in the majors, or GF with clubs.
| **2♦** | Either 0-7 with 5+ hearts and 3 or fewer spades, or GF with diamonds.
| **2♥** | Either 0-7 with 5+ spades and 3 or fewer hearts, or GF with 5-4/4-5 or better in the minors.
| **2♠** | invitational to 3NT, asks specifically about a spade stopper, suggests shortness. |
| **2NT** | invitational to 3NT, asks specifically about a heart stopper, suggests shortness. |
| **3m** | Invitational, 6+ in the minor. |
| **3M** | Solid 7-card corresponding minor (wants partner to declare 3NT). |
| **3NT** | To play. |
| **4NT / 5NT** | Invitational to 6 / 7. |

### Puppet Followups - General Structure

2♣ is not quite a puppet, but asks opener to bids 2♥ with four hearts and 2♦ otherwise. 2♦ and 2♥ are actual puppets, opener bids the next step.

After 2♣-2♦, responder rebids a 5 card major with a weak majors hand.
After other puppets, responder passes with the weak hand with the major.

All other followups are game forcing with 6+ in the minor (2♣/2♦) or 5-4/4-5 in the minors (2♥). Responder shows shape next, and opener either places the contract (usually in 3NT) or bids cooperatively to investigate slam. 

### 2♣ Followups 

Opener shows four hearts by bidding 2♥, and otherwise denies four hearts by completing the puppet to 2♦.

If opener shows 4 hearts, responder passes with a weak majors hand. Otherwise over 2♦ he rebids a 5 card major to show a weak hand with both majors. All other followups are game forcing with 6+ clubs.

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **2♥** | 0-5, at least 5♥ and 4♠. Signoff. |
| **2♠** | 0-5, at least 4♥ and 5♠. Signoff. |
| **2NT** | GF, 6+ exactly clubs, no shortness. |
| **3♣/♦** | GF, 6+ clubs, shortness, cheaper=lower |
| **3♥/♠** | GF, 6+ clubs, spade singleton/void |

### 2♦ Followups

This is a puppet. Opener bids 2♥ (completing the puppet). Responder's rebid:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **pass** | 0-7, 5+ hearts and 3 or fewer spades |
| **2NT** | GF, 6 exactly diamonds, no shortness. |
| **2♠/3♣** | GF, 6+ diamonds, shortness, cheaper=lower |
| **3♦/3♥** | GF, 6+ diamonds, spade singleton/void |

In both of these sequences, after responder shows shortness, opener can relay to get exact shape (there are no outside 4 card suits!), 3NT by opener is always to play, and otherwise opener cuebids cooperatively investigating slam.

### 2♥ Followups

This is also a puppet. Opener bids 2♠ (completing the puppet). Responder's rebid, if not pass, shows GF with at least 5-4/4-5 in the minors:

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **pass** | Weak hand with 0-7, 5+ hearts and 3 or fewer spades | 
| **2NT** | GF, 2245 or 2254 shape. (relay to ask) |
| **3m** | GF, this minor longer with a shortness, relay is Two Suited Shortness Ask (see Shortness Ask document for more) |
| **3M** | 5-5 with shortness in this major. |
| **3NT** | 6-6 in the minors |

## §8 — 1♣-1♦-3M / 3NT / 4M

**3M (~4.5 losers, 6+M):** Stronger than a 2M rebid but short of Namyats — perhaps in length or suit quality. Responder raises, bids 3NT, or splinters.

**3NT (Gambling):** Long running minor plus one outside stopper. Pass or pull.

**4M (To play):** Stronger than Namyats — opener could have bid 4m (Namyats) but chose not to because the hand has too many tricks for that treatment.

## §9 — Fourth Seat Interference over 1♣-1♦

Since responder denied a 4cM with 8+ HCP, a major-suit game is unlikely. Competing in majors is not a priority.

**General principles:**
- Double of a natural suit bid below 3NT = takeout/negative.
- Over **(1M)** interference: opener rebids as if without the interference. Still try to bid 1NT with a stopper and 15–17 balanced.
- Over **(2m)** interference: opener should have more values to bid 2M than 3m, because of the negative inferences from responder's 1♦ bid.

**If opener passes:**
- Responder's jump-shift into a minor = natural and game-forcing.
- Non-jump minor-suit bids = constructive but non-forcing.

**If the opponents find a fit:**
- 2NT by responder = takeout for the minors.
- If responder balances with a double, opener should prioritize bidding NT with a stopper over showing 4 cards in the other major.
